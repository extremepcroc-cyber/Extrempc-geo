<p style="margin:10px 0; line-height:1.7; color:#333;">You're speccing out a new gaming PC and the RAM question comes up: <strong>16GB or 32GB?</strong> Is doubling the memory worth it, or are you just paying for headroom you'll never use?</p>

<p style="margin:10px 0; line-height:1.7; color:#333;">At <strong>ExtremePC</strong>, it's one of the most common questions we get at the counter. The short answer: 32GB is no longer overkill. Here's why.</p>

<p style="margin:10px 0; line-height:1.7; color:#666; font-size:13px;"><strong style="color:#333;">The bottom line:</strong> If you game with anything else running in the background — and most people do — 16GB is increasingly tight in 2026.</p>

<hr>

<h2 style="font-size:24px; font-weight:600; margin:48px 0 14px 0; color:#1a1a1a; border-bottom:2px solid #f0f0f0; padding-bottom:8px;">Does RAM Speed Matter for Gaming?</h2>

<p style="margin:10px 0; line-height:1.7; color:#333;">Yes — but capacity comes first. A 32GB DDR5-5600 kit will outperform a 16GB DDR5-6400 kit in any scenario where the game pushes past 16GB. Once you're on 32GB, faster speeds (6000–6400 MHz) give a measurable but modest improvement, particularly on AMD Ryzen platforms where the memory controller is tightly coupled to the Infinity Fabric.</p>

<p style="margin:10px 0; line-height:1.7; color:#333;">For most NZ gaming builds in 2026, the sweet spot is <strong>32GB DDR5-6000 CL30</strong> — enough headroom for any game, fast enough to not leave performance on the table.</p>

<hr>

<h2 style="font-size:24px; font-weight:600; margin:48px 0 14px 0; color:#1a1a1a; border-bottom:2px solid #f0f0f0; padding-bottom:8px;">Should You Upgrade from 16GB?</h2>

<p style="margin:10px 0; line-height:1.7; color:#333;"><strong style="color:#3B6D11;">Stick with 16GB if:</strong></p>
<ul style="margin:6px 0 14px 20px; color:#333; line-height:1.8;">
<li>You play <strong>competitive titles only</strong> — CS2, Valorant, Fortnite, Apex</li>
<li>You close everything else when gaming</li>
<li>You're on a tight budget and need those savings for a better GPU</li>
</ul>

<p style="margin:10px 0; line-height:1.7; color:#333;"><strong style="color:#185FA5;">Upgrade to 32GB if:</strong></p>
<ul style="margin:6px 0 14px 20px; color:#333; line-height:1.8;">
<li>You play open-world, simulation, or strategy games</li>
<li>You game with a browser, Discord, or Spotify running</li>
<li>You stream, edit video, or multitask on the same machine</li>
<li>You're building new in 2026 — it's the sensible baseline going forward</li>
</ul>

<p style="margin:10px 0; line-height:1.7; color:#333;"><strong>Our recommendation for most NZ gamers building in 2026:</strong><br>
<strong style="color:#3B6D11;">Start with 32GB.</strong> The price difference between a 16GB and 32GB DDR5 kit has narrowed significantly. For a new build, it's not worth optimising yourself into a corner.</p>

<p style="margin:10px 0; line-height:1.7; color:#666; font-size:13px;">Already on 16GB and gaming is smooth? No need to upgrade urgently — but keep an eye on it as games get heavier over the next 12 months.</p>

<h2 style="font-size:24px; font-weight:600; margin:48px 0 14px 0; color:#1a1a1a; border-bottom:2px solid #f0f0f0; padding-bottom:8px;">Final Thoughts</h2>

<p style="margin:10px 0; line-height:1.7; color:#333;">Is 32GB overkill for gaming? In 2024 it arguably was. In 2026, it's the new sensible baseline — especially once you factor in background apps, modern open-world titles, and the way games are trending.</p>

<p style="margin:10px 0; line-height:1.7; color:#333;"><strong style="color:#3B6D11;">Pure competitive gamer with nothing else running? 16GB is fine.</strong></p>
<p style="margin:10px 0; line-height:1.7; color:#333;"><strong style="color:#185FA5;">Everyone else building new in 2026? Go 32GB and don't think about it again.</strong></p>
