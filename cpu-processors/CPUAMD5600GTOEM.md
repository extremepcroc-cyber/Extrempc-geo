## FAQ

- **Q: Is the GT significantly faster than the original 5600G?**
  A: ~3–8% in gaming and productivity. The GT is the better chip if pricing is close. If the original 5600G is meaningfully cheaper, the savings might be worth more than the small uplift.

- **Q: Can I really game on the integrated graphics?**
  A: For 1080p competitive games (Valorant, CS2, Rocket League, League of Legends, Apex Legends low settings), yes — 80+ fps achievable. For modern AAA at 1080p high (Cyberpunk, Starfield, Hogwarts Legacy), no — these need a discrete GPU. The iGPU buys time to save up for a real GPU.

- **Q: Will Auckland heat cause throttling?**
  A: At 65W TDP, no — a good tower cooler keeps the chip cool even in 28°C+ ambient. Vega 7 iGPU can push temps higher when gaming on it; ensure case airflow is reasonable.

- **Q: BIOS update — what's involved?**
  A: Newer B550 motherboards likely have current BIOS out of the box. Older B450 / X470 boards may need a flash. ExtremePC's services team can do this in-store (small fee). Email support@extremepc.co.nz before ordering if unsure.

- **Q: How long can I keep this PC before needing a major upgrade?**
  A: 3–5 years for office/productivity use, easily. For gaming, the CPU stays sufficient for 1080p 60Hz at high settings paired with a mid-tier discrete GPU. The platform is the bottleneck eventually — AM4 won't host new chips beyond what's currently available.
