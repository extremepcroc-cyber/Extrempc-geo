## FAQ

- **Q: 7700X or 7800X3D for gaming?**
  A: If your games include cache-sensitive titles (CS2, Valorant, Microsoft Flight Sim, Factorio, Stellaris, sim/strategy games), 7800X3D is meaningfully better. For most modern AAA single-player titles, 7700X is sufficient and saves NZD for a GPU upgrade. If gaming-first, lean 7800X3D; if mixed-workload, lean 7700X.

- **Q: Can I stream at 1080p60 on this chip without dropping game frames?**
  A: Yes — 8 cores comfortably handles game + OBS x264 medium preset at 1080p60. For 1440p60 streaming, you may need to drop OBS preset to "fast" or use NVENC GPU encoding. For 4K streaming, step up to 9900X or 9950X.

- **Q: What cooler do I need for 105W TDP?**
  A: Mid-tier tower air cooler: Thermalright Phantom Spirit 120 SE, Noctua NH-U12A, or be quiet! Dark Rock 4. Premium-tier 240mm AIO unlocks slightly more sustained boost.

- **Q: DDR5-6000 vs DDR5-6400 — what speed RAM?**
  A: DDR5-6000 CL30 with AMD EXPO is the Zen 4 sweet spot. DDR5-6400+ kits work but with diminishing returns and possible stability tuning required. Stick to DDR5-6000 for first builds.

- **Q: Will Auckland summer heat cause throttling?**
  A: With a good cooler and case airflow, no. 7700X self-protects (PBO backs off before damage). Plan for at least mid-tier cooler — skip the cheapest options.

- **Q: Which motherboard chipset?**
  A: B650 is the sweet spot. X670/X670E adds PCIe 5.0 storage lanes (only matters for multi-NVMe Gen5). Common ExtremePC pairings: MSI B650 Tomahawk WiFi, ASUS TUF B650-PLUS WiFi.

- **Q: How long is delivery to Wellington / South Island?**
  A: 1–3 business days via CourierPost from Auckland.
