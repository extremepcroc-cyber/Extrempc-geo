- **vs Intel Core i5-12400F / 13400F (similar tier Intel)** — Intel i5-12400F and 13400F are 6-core competitors. 5700X has 2 more cores for modern AAA / productivity / streaming. Intel platform is also end-of-life with no upgrade path. 5700X is the broader choice at this tier.

- **vs Ryzen 7 5800X (similar AM4 tier, higher TDP)** — 5800X is the 105W version of essentially the same silicon — slightly higher boost behavior, runs hotter, needs better cooling. For users with a high-end cooler already, 5800X delivers ~3–5% more performance. For new builds, 5700X's 65W is the more efficient choice.

## FAQ

- **Q: I have a Ryzen 5 3600 on a B450 motherboard. Is upgrading to the 5700X worth it?**
  A: For modern AAA gaming (Unreal Engine 5 titles especially), yes — the 8-core uplift is measurable. For older games or 1080p mid-settings, the upgrade is smaller. If your GPU is the bottleneck (sub-RTX 4060), upgrade the GPU first.

- **Q: Do I need a BIOS update on my B450 board?**
  A: Almost certainly yes. AGESA 1.2.0.0+ is required for 5000-series support. Most B450 boards from 2020+ have current BIOS; older units need a flash. ExtremePC's services team handles this in-store if needed.

- **Q: What cooler should I use?**
  A: A mid-tier tower cooler handles 65W TDP easily. Thermalright Peerless Assassin 120 SE or ID-COOLING SE-224-XTS are popular ExtremePC pairings. No AIO needed at this TDP.

- **Q: Will my existing AM4 cooler work?**
  A: Yes — AM4 socket is unchanged. Any cooler that fits AM4 (Wraith Stealth from older builds, aftermarket coolers) will work physically. Check that it's rated for at least 65W TDP.

- **Q: 5700X vs 5800X3D — which for gaming?**
  A: 5800X3D is the AM4 gaming king (with 3D V-Cache) and outperforms 5700X by 10–25% in cache-sensitive games. But 5800X3D is end-of-life and increasingly hard to find. If you can find a 5800X3D in stock and the gaming-focused premium is acceptable, get that. Otherwise, 5700X is the easier-to-source 8-core choice.
