## FAQ

- **Q: F-suffix means no iGPU — what if my discrete GPU dies?**
  A: You won't see a display until the GPU is replaced. If this concerns you, get the 14400 (non-F) with Intel UHD 730 iGPU.

- **Q: 14400F or 12400F — which?**
  A: 14400F adds 4 E-cores for multitasking and streaming. For pure gaming with no background apps, 12400F saves NZD. For streamers or users with many apps running, 14400F.

- **Q: Was 14400F affected by i9 stability issues?**
  A: No. The Intel voltage/oxidation issue specifically affected i9 high-power chips, not i5 14400F.

- **Q: DDR4 or DDR5 motherboard?**
  A: For 14400F specifically, DDR4-3200 is fully sufficient. If you have existing DDR4 RAM, use it. If buying new RAM, DDR5 future-proofs but adds cost.

- **Q: What cooler do you recommend?**
  A: Entry-tier tower air cooler (ID-COOLING SE-224-XTS, Thermalright Assassin X120 R-SE) handles 14400F's 65W TDP with low noise. AIO unnecessary.

- **Q: How do P-cores and E-cores affect my workflow?**
  A: Windows 11 Thread Director assigns gaming and foreground apps to fast P-cores; background tasks (Discord, browser refresh, Windows Update) to E-cores. No configuration needed — you'll see smoother multitasking automatically.

- **Q: Can I stream 1080p60 from this chip?**
  A: Yes, using GPU NVENC for encoding while CPU handles the game. Quick Sync alternative also works. For 1440p60 or 4K streaming, step up to 14700F.

- **Q: How long is delivery to Wellington / South Island?**
  A: 1–3 business days via CourierPost from Auckland.
