## FAQ

- **Q: 8400F vs 7500F — which should I pick?**
  A: Pick by price at the moment. Performance is within ~5% on most workloads. 7500F has larger cache (better for cache-sensitive games like Stellaris, Factorio); 8400F has higher base clock (better light-load responsiveness). Both run AM5, both have no iGPU.

- **Q: Monolithic die — what does that mean for me as a user?**
  A: AMD's 8000-series uses a single silicon die instead of chiplet design used in 7000-series. Lower latency on lightly-threaded workloads, slightly lower idle power. The downside is smaller L3 cache. For most users, this is a marginal architectural difference, not a deal-breaker either direction.

- **Q: Will Auckland heat cause throttling?**
  A: At 65W TDP, no. A good tower cooler handles 8400F easily in NZ ambient temperatures.

- **Q: What's the cheapest motherboard that works?**
  A: B650 is the entry-recommended chipset for AM5. ASUS Prime B650-PLUS or MSI B650M PRO are common entry-tier choices.

- **Q: First AM5 build — what should I watch out for?**
  A: DDR5 memory training on first boot takes 1–3 minutes (no screen during that time — be patient). EXPO profile in BIOS enables advertised RAM speed. Make sure CPU power cable (8-pin EPS) is fully seated; AM5 motherboards often have 8+4 or dual 8-pin EPS slots.
