## FAQ

- **Q: 9850X3D or 7800X3D — should I pay the premium?**
  A: For pure gaming, 7800X3D is still extraordinary value at meaningfully lower NZD — only ~5–10% behind 9850X3D in gaming. The 9850X3D wins on productivity (~20% better), latest architecture, higher base clock, and longer software support timeline. If gaming-only on tight budget, 7800X3D. If you want the future-leaning best-of-both, 9850X3D.

- **Q: 9850X3D for productivity — does the X3D hurt productivity?**
  A: Older X3D chips (5800X3D, 7800X3D) had lower base clocks that hurt productivity. 9850X3D's 4.7 GHz base fixes this — productivity is competitive with non-X3D Zen 5 chips. The trade-off that existed in earlier X3D generations is largely gone.

- **Q: What cooler is required?**
  A: 240mm AIO or premium-tier tower air. The X3D layer adds thermal complexity — Thermalright Phantom Spirit 120 EVO, Noctua NH-D15, or 240mm AIO (Arctic Liquid Freezer III, NZXT Kraken 240). Premium 240mm AIO is the recommended pairing for full sustained boost.

- **Q: Will Auckland summer heat affect X3D performance?**
  A: With 240mm AIO and good case airflow, no. 9850X3D self-protects (PBO backs off before damage). Plan cooling for 120W TDP — don't underspec.

- **Q: Which motherboard for 9850X3D?**
  A: X670E or X870E recommended. B650 works but may not fully push the chip. Common pairings: ASUS ROG STRIX X670E-E, MSI MAG X670E Tomahawk WiFi, Gigabyte X870E AORUS Elite.

- **Q: DDR5 speed for X3D Zen 5?**
