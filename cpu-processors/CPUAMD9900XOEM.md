## FAQ

- **Q: 9900X or 7700X — which for streaming?**
  A: For 1080p60 streaming, 7700X is sufficient and saves NZD. For 1440p60 or 4K streaming, the 9900X's 4 extra cores prevent OBS from competing with game frames for CPU time. If streaming is a serious workload, lean 9900X.

- **Q: 9900X or 9950X — when do I need 16 cores?**
  A: 16 cores benefits Blender, Cinebench multi-thread, video render farms, large code compiles, and parallel ML/data processing. For 12-core-or-less workloads (most productivity), 9900X is sufficient. The 16-core step up is meaningful for professional content creation and ML developers.

- **Q: 9900X or 9950X3D for gaming?**
  A: 9950X3D wins gaming by 10–25% in cache-sensitive titles thanks to 3D V-Cache. 9900X wins on productivity multi-thread (similar core count without X3D's thermal compromise). If gaming-first, 9950X3D. If productivity-first, 9900X.

- **Q: What cooler do I need for 120W TDP?**
  A: Premium-tier tower air or 240mm AIO. Thermalright Phantom Spirit 120 EVO, Noctua NH-D15, Arctic Liquid Freezer III 240, or NZXT Kraken 240. Entry-tier budget air coolers will throttle under sustained load.

- **Q: Will Auckland summer heat affect 12 cores under load?**
  A: With a 240mm AIO or premium tower air and good case airflow, no. PBO self-protects (boosts back off before thermal damage). Plan cooling appropriately — don't underspec for 12-core 120W chip.

- **Q: Which motherboard for 9900X?**
  A: X670 or X870 is recommended for 12+ core chips — better VRMs for sustained boost behavior. B650 works but may not fully push the chip under heavy load. Common pairings: ASUS ROG STRIX X670E-A, MSI MAG X670E Tomahawk, Gigabyte X670 AORUS Elite.

- **Q: DDR5 speed for serious productivity?**
  A: DDR5-6000 CL30 EXPO remains the sweet spot for AM5. For content creation workloads, prioritize capacity (64GB) over speed (6400+). 64GB DDR5-6000 CL30 is the productivity sweet spot.
