## FAQ

- **Q: 9950X or 9950X3D — which for content creation?**
  A: For productivity-dominant workloads (Blender, code compilation, data processing), 9950X. The 3D V-Cache on 9950X3D helps gaming but slightly reduces sustained productivity throughput due to thermal characteristics. For mixed gaming + productivity, 9950X3D is justified.

- **Q: 9950X or 9900X — when is 16 cores worth it?**
  A: 16 cores benefits Blender renders (~25–40% faster than 12), Cinebench multi-thread (~30% faster), large parallel compilation jobs. For 12-core-or-less workloads, 9900X is sufficient and saves NZD. The step up is meaningful for serious content creation and ML.

- **Q: What cooler is required for 170W TDP?**
  A: 280mm or 360mm AIO recommended. Premium tower air (Noctua NH-D15, Thermalright Phantom Spirit 120 EVO) works but with thermal headroom limited under sustained max load. For best behavior: Arctic Liquid Freezer III 280/360, NZXT Kraken 280/360 Elite. Budget air coolers will throttle.

- **Q: Will Auckland summer heat be an issue?**
  A: With 280mm+ AIO and good case airflow, no. 9950X self-protects via PBO. Plan cooling proportional to the chip — don't underspec.

- **Q: Which motherboard for 9950X?**
  A: X670E or X870E is recommended for 16-core flagship — robust VRMs for sustained 170W TDP without thermal throttling. Common pairings: ASUS ROG STRIX X670E-A, MSI MEG X670E ACE, Gigabyte X670E AORUS MASTER.

- **Q: 32GB vs 64GB vs 96GB DDR5 — what's right for 9950X?**
  A: 64GB is the productivity sweet spot for the chip's positioning. 32GB is sufficient for gaming + light productivity but doesn't fully use the chip's potential. 96GB+ benefits ML, VFX, and very large dataset work. DDR5-6000 CL30 EXPO is the recommended speed.

- **Q: Does this chip game well?**
  A: Yes, very well — 9950X matches or slightly trails 7800X3D in most modern AAA gaming due to high boost clocks compensating for lack of X3D. Where the 16 cores shine is streaming + gaming, or productivity that benefits from many cores.
