## FAQ

- **Q: 265K vs 14700F — should I jump to LGA1851?**
  A: 265K is forward-leaning: AI NPU, TSMC 3nm efficiency, current socket roadmap. 14700F is mature LGA1700 with proven track record at slightly lower NZD. If you value AI features and forward platform, 265K. If you prioritize value and don't need AI NPU, 14700F.

- **Q: K-suffix overclocking — do I need to overclock?**
  A: No. K-suffix means it's *capable* of overclocking, not that you must. Stock performance is already strong. Overclocking unlocks ~5–10% more sustained performance with proper cooling and tuning — for enthusiasts only.

- **Q: AI NPU — what should I expect from 33 TOPS?**
  A: Meaningful acceleration for: Stable Diffusion image generation, local LLM inference (7B–13B parameter models), Windows Copilot+ features, Adobe AI features (background removal, denoising), real-time AI video effects. Not enough to replace a dedicated GPU for heavy AI training, but plenty for everyday AI-augmented workflows.

- **Q: What cooler do I need at 125W base / 250W max turbo?**
  A: 240mm AIO minimum; 280mm/360mm AIO recommended for sustained turbo behavior. Premium tower air (Noctua NH-D15, Thermalright Phantom Spirit 120 EVO) works for non-overclocked builds. For overclocking, 360mm AIO is the recommendation.

- **Q: Which motherboard chipset?**
  A: Z890 is recommended for K-suffix chips — supports overclocking. B860 works for non-OC use cases at lower cost. Common Z890 pairings: ASUS ROG STRIX Z890-A Gaming WiFi, MSI MAG Z890 Tomahawk WiFi.

- **Q: DDR5 RAM — what speed?**
  A: DDR5-6400 CL32 is the sweet spot (official max support). DDR5-6000 CL30 is fine and saves some cost. 32GB recommended; 64GB for serious content creation or AI work.

- **Q: ECC memory supported?**
  A: Yes. Core Ultra 200 supports ECC DDR5 with compatible motherboards. Useful for workstation reliability — though most consumer setups use non-ECC.

- **Q: Was 265K affected by 13th/14th Gen issues?**
  A: No. 265K is current Arrow Lake architecture on TSMC 3nm — a different chip family from the affected 13th/14th Gen products. The Intel voltage/oxidation issue does not apply.
