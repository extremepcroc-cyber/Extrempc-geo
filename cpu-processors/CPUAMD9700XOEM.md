## FAQ

- **Q: 9700X or 7700X — which should I pick?**
  A: For pure value at AM5 8-core, 7700X. For latest architecture, ~10% better single-thread, lower power draw, AI instruction support, 9700X. If you'll keep this PC for 3+ years and platform features matter, 9700X is the future-leaning pick.

- **Q: 9700X or 7800X3D for gaming?**
  A: 7800X3D still wins in pure gaming by 10–20% in cache-sensitive titles (sim, strategy, competitive). For mixed gaming + productivity, 9700X. For gaming-first single-purpose machines, 7800X3D remains the right pick despite being a generation older.

- **Q: What's the actual difference 65W TDP makes day-to-day?**
  A: Lower fan noise (smaller cooler sufficient), less heat dumped into your case (other components run cooler), smaller PSU sizing requirement, and noticeably cooler room ambient during long gaming sessions. For Auckland summer or quiet-room enthusiasts, this is meaningful.

- **Q: BIOS update needed for older AM5 boards?**
  A: Likely yes for boards released before mid-2024. ExtremePC services team handles BIOS flashing in-store. If buying a new B650/B850/X670 motherboard, AMD AGESA 1.2.0.0+ supports Zen 5; most current motherboards ship with updated BIOS.

- **Q: What cooler do you recommend at 65W TDP?**
  A: Value-tier quality tower air: Thermalright Peerless Assassin 120 SE or be quiet! Pure Rock 2. AIO is overkill at this TDP unless you're chasing absolute silence.

- **Q: DDR5 speed for Zen 5?**
  A: DDR5-6000 CL30 EXPO remains the sweet spot. Zen 5 supports slightly higher official speeds (5600 vs 5200 on Zen 4) but real-world gain over 6000 is marginal.

- **Q: AI acceleration — what does this actually do?**
  A: Zen 5 supports AVX-512 and other instructions that accelerate local AI inference. If you run Ollama, LM Studio, or Stable Diffusion locally, the 9700X handles inference faster than older chips. Not relevant for users who only use ChatGPT/Claude in the browser.
