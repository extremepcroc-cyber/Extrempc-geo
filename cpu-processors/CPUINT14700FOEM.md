## FAQ

- **Q: 14700F OEM vs 14700F retail — which?**
  A: Same chip, same Intel warranty. OEM tray (this SKU) saves NZD. Retail boxed version (CPUINT14700F) adds the printed box. If you don't care about the box, OEM is the value pick.

- **Q: Was 14700F affected by the i9 / i7 degradation issues?**
  A: Partially. Intel issued microcode patches in mid-2024 that resolved the issue for i7-13700K and i7-14700K (the unlocked-K variants drawing high power). The 14700F is the locked F variant with lower max turbo power (219W vs unlocked's 253W+), and benefits from the same microcode fix. ExtremePC ships current production silicon with patched microcode. If you ever experience instability, BIOS update to latest version typically resolves.

- **Q: F-suffix means no iGPU — backup options?**
  A: F-suffix = no display output without discrete GPU. Quick Sync video acceleration still works for encoding, just no display. If you want iGPU safety net, the non-F i7-14700 was available but not currently in our stock; ask Build Team for ETA.

- **Q: What cooler do I need at 219W max turbo?**
  A: Premium-tier tower air or 240mm AIO recommended. Thermalright Phantom Spirit 120 EVO, Noctua NH-D15, or Arctic Liquid Freezer III 240. Skip entry-tier coolers — they'll throttle the chip under sustained load.

- **Q: DDR4 or DDR5 for 14700F?**
  A: DDR5-5600 is the better match for the chip's productivity potential. DDR4-3200 works but gives up some throughput on multi-threaded workloads. For new builds, DDR5; for DDR4 RAM reuse, the 14700F still performs well.

- **Q: Will Auckland summer heat be an issue?**
  A: With a 240mm AIO or premium tower air and good case airflow, no. 219W max turbo is serious — plan cooling proportional to the chip.

- **Q: Which motherboard chipset?**
  A: Z790 is the recommended chipset for 14700F — robust VRMs for sustained 219W turbo. B760 works for moderate workloads but may not push the chip to max under sustained load. Common ExtremePC pairings: ASUS TUF Gaming Z790-Plus WiFi, MSI MAG Z790 Tomahawk WiFi.
