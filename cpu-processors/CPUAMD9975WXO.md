## FAQ

- **Q: Do I actually need 32 cores? When is Threadripper PRO justified?**
  A: If your workload involves Blender renders, CAD/CAE simulations, ML training, video render farms, or large-scale parallel data processing, and you currently use a desktop chip and feel core-count limited — yes. If you're "future-proofing" without specific use case, no. 9950X3D handles ~95% of mixed productivity at much lower cost.

- **Q: Can I game on this?**
  A: Yes, but it's not the best gaming chip — 8-channel memory subsystem adds latency vs 2-channel mainstream desktop, and X3D-style 3D V-Cache isn't currently available on Threadripper. Gaming is fine (matches mainstream chip frame rates roughly), but you're paying for workstation features you won't use. For gaming-first builds, get 9950X3D or 9850X3D.

- **Q: What's required for a complete 9975WX build?**
  A: sTR5 motherboard (WRX90 SAGE or TRX50 AERO D), 4×16GB or 4×32GB DDR5 ECC kit (8-channel population recommended), 420mm AIO or custom loop cooling, 1200W+ Platinum/Titanium PSU, workstation GPU (RTX 5090 or A6000 class). Total system build typically lands at the upper workstation tier. Build Team can spec the full system.

- **Q: ECC RAM — do I need it?**
  A: For professional use (scientific, financial, large-scale rendering), yes — ECC prevents silent data corruption that can cost hours of work or invalidate results. For general professional content creation, recommended but not strictly required.
