## FAQ

- **Q: 7600X vs 7700X — which for gaming?**
  A: For 1080p–1440p gaming with mid-range GPUs (RTX 4060, RX 7600), 7600X is sufficient. The 7700X's 2 extra cores show benefit in modern AAA games using Unreal Engine 5 (Stalker 2, Black Myth Wukong), streaming workloads, and productivity. If pure gaming with mid-range GPU, save NZD on 7600X. If future-proofing or mixed workload, 7700X.

- **Q: Can I game on the integrated graphics?**
  A: No. The 7600X's iGPU is for display output during desktop use, not gaming. It can't run modern games even at low settings. Treat it as a troubleshooting fallback, not a gaming option.

- **Q: What cooler do I need for 105W TDP?**
  A: A mid-tier tower air cooler: Thermalright Phantom Spirit 120 SE, Noctua NH-U12A, or be quiet! Dark Rock 4. Premium air or 240mm AIO unlocks full sustained boost. Skip the cheapest budget coolers — they throttle the chip under sustained load.

- **Q: Will Auckland summer heat cause throttling?**
  A: With a good cooler and case airflow, no. 7600X has thermal headroom under proper cooling. If ambient hits 30°C+ and your case airflow is poor, you'll see boost back off, but the chip self-protects (PBO backs off before damage).

- **Q: Which motherboard chipset?**
  A: B650 is the sweet spot for 7600X. X670/X670E adds extra PCIe 5.0 storage lanes (only matters for multi-NVMe Gen5 setups). A620 works but limits overclocking. Common ExtremePC pairings: ASUS TUF B650-PLUS WiFi, MSI B650 Tomahawk WiFi.

- **Q: DDR5-6000 vs DDR5-5200 — which RAM speed?**
  A: DDR5-6000 CL30 with AMD EXPO is the sweet spot for Zen 4 — modest performance uplift over base 5200. Going higher than 6000 has diminishing returns and may require manual tuning. Recommended: 32GB DDR5-6000 CL30 EXPO kit.
