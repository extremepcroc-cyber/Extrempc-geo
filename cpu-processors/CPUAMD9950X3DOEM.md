## FAQ

- **Q: 9950X3D or 9850X3D — which for gaming?**
  A: Both gaming performance is essentially identical (3D V-Cache is the gaming differentiator, not core count). 9850X3D wins on gaming-pure NZD value. 9950X3D wins if you also want 16-core productivity. For gaming-only flagship builds, 9850X3D. For gaming + content creation, 9950X3D.

- **Q: 9950X3D or 9950X — is the X3D worth it?**
  A: For productivity-only workloads, 9950X is the value win and saves meaningful NZD. For users who also play CPU-sensitive games (CS2 competitive, MSFS, sim/strategy), the X3D's gaming uplift is meaningful (15–25%). If gaming is a serious secondary workload, 9950X3D.

- **Q: What cooler is required for 170W TDP + X3D?**
  A: 280mm or 360mm AIO recommended. Premium tower air can handle it but with thermal headroom limited under sustained max load. Recommended: Arctic Liquid Freezer III 360, NZXT Kraken 360 Elite, Corsair iCUE H150i Elite LCD. Skip budget coolers.

- **Q: Does 9950X3D have the dual-CCD X3D issue from 7950X3D?**
  A: 7950X3D had 8 cores with X3D + 8 cores without — Windows scheduling could send games to the wrong CCD. 9950X3D's architecture handles scheduling better, and AMD's chipset driver has matured. Real-world gaming performance is consistent. ExtremePC's Build Team can advise on BIOS/Windows settings for optimal scheduling if you're concerned.

- **Q: Which motherboard for 9950X3D?**
  A: X670E or X870E strongly recommended — robust VRMs essential for sustained 170W TDP. Common pairings: ASUS ROG STRIX X670E-E, MSI MEG X670E ACE, Gigabyte X870E AORUS MASTER.

- **Q: 32GB or 64GB DDR5?**
  A: For gaming + light productivity, 32GB DDR5-6000 CL30 EXPO is fine. For serious content creation or ML, 64GB. For VFX and very large datasets, 96GB+. The chip handles up to DDR5-8000 with manual tuning, but 6000 CL30 EXPO is the proven sweet spot.

- **Q: Will Auckland summer heat be an issue?**
  A: With 360mm AIO and good case airflow, no. 9950X3D self-protects via PBO. Plan cooling proportional to the chip.
