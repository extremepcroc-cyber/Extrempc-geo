## Comparison

- **vs Ryzen 5 5600G (CPUAMD5600GOEM, AM4 with iGPU)** — 5600G includes Radeon Vega 7 iGPU; 5600X has no graphics. If you already have a discrete GPU or are buying one, 5600X is the better gaming chip (higher clocks, more cache). If you can't afford a GPU yet, 5600G is the only one that boots a display.

- **vs Ryzen 7 5700X (CPUAMD5700XOEM, AM4 8-core upgrade)** — 5700X adds 2 more cores (8 total / 16 threads) for productivity and modern engines. For pure 1080p gaming under 144Hz, 5600X is sufficient. For 1440p / 165Hz+ gaming, mixed productivity, or upgrade headroom, 5700X is the better long-term choice.

- **vs Ryzen 5 7600X (CPUAMD7600XOEM, AM5 mid-tier)** — 7600X is the current AM5 platform — DDR5, PCIe 5.0, multi-year upgrade path. But total build cost lands meaningfully higher due to AM5 motherboard + DDR5 RAM premium. For absolute budget builds, 5600X wins on $/frame. For future-proof builds, 7600X.

- **vs Intel Core i5-12400F / 13400F (mid-tier Intel budget)** — Intel i5-12400F is the closest direct competitor — 6 cores / 12 threads, similar 1080p gaming performance. Intel LGA1700 is end-of-life with no upgrade path. AMD AM4 is also end-of-life but mobo+RAM savings on AM4 are deeper. Roughly even on $/frame; choose whichever has a better total-build deal at the moment.

- **vs Ryzen 5 5500 (lower-tier AM4 budget)** — 5500 is the cheaper non-X variant but uses Zen 3 with only PCIe 3.0 and reduced cache. For a small step up in NZD, 5600X gives meaningfully better gaming and full PCIe 4.0 support — worth the small premium.

## FAQ

- **Q: Is the 5600X still a good chip in 2026?**
  A: For 1080p high-refresh gaming and budget builds, yes. It's not the latest, but the gaming bottleneck for most users is the GPU, not a 5600X. If you're pairing with a sub-RTX 4070 GPU, the CPU won't be your bottleneck.

- **Q: What's the cheapest motherboard that works well?**
  A: An entry-tier B550 motherboard (ASUS Prime B550M-A, Gigabyte B550M DS3H, MSI B550M PRO-VDH) is the sweet spot. B450 also works with BIOS update if you want to save more. Avoid A320 — too old.

- **Q: What cooler do you recommend?**
  A: ID-COOLING SE-224-XTS or Thermalright Assassin X120 R-SE — both entry-tier and handle 5600X's 65W TDP with low noise. AIO is unnecessary at this TDP.

- **Q: Will my old DDR4 RAM work?**
  A: Yes, any DDR4 kit (3000 MT/s minimum recommended, 3200 ideal). Speeds above 3200 work but with diminishing returns on AM4 platform.

- **Q: AM4 is dying — should I buy AM5 instead?**
  A: Depends on your budget timeline. If you build today at value-tier total spend and don't plan to upgrade for 5+ years, AM4 + 5600X is the value win. If you plan to upgrade chips in 2–3 years, AM5 platform with 7500F or 7600X is the better start.
