## Comparison

- **vs Ryzen 5 5600GT (CPUAMD5600GTOEM, slightly premium-tier AM4 iGPU)** — 5600GT is the refreshed 5600G with slightly higher clocks (3.6 GHz base vs 3.9 GHz on G — wait, the GT is actually 3.6 vs G's 3.9 base, but the GT's boost reaches similar). In practice, performance difference is small (3–5%); the GT is the better choice if both are available, but the G remains a value at lower NZD.

- **vs Ryzen 5 5600X (CPUAMD5600XOEM, similar AM4 tier without iGPU)** — 5600X has no integrated graphics — you must add a discrete GPU. If GPU cost is in the budget, 5600X gives meaningfully better gaming performance at similar NZD. If GPU cost is *not* in the budget yet, 5600G is the only option that boots a display.

- **vs Ryzen 5 7500F (CPUAMD7500FOEM, AM5 budget no iGPU)** — 7500F is on the current AM5 platform with DDR5 and PCIe 5.0 — a meaningfully more future-proof investment. But the total build cost (AM5 motherboard + DDR5) lands much higher than AM4 + 5600G. For absolute budget builds and HTPC use, 5600G remains the right call. For users planning to upgrade the platform in 3–5 years, 7500F is the better starting point.

- **vs Intel Core i3-12100 / i3-13100 (gaming-tier Intel budget)** — Intel i3-12100 is a strong 4-core / 8-thread budget chip. Slightly faster in single-thread gaming. But Intel i3 chips at this price don't have a useful iGPU for gaming (UHD 730 is display-only). For builders who genuinely need iGPU gaming, 5600G has no real Intel competitor under this NZD tier.

- **vs Ryzen 5 5600 (non-G, similar AM4 budget without iGPU)** — The non-G "regular" 5600 is the entry-tier choice when paired with a budget discrete GPU. For pure CPU gaming performance with a GPU, 5600 is slightly better than 5600G. 5600G's iGPU is the only reason to choose it.

## FAQ

- **Q: Can I really game on the integrated graphics?**
  A: Yes, with realistic expectations. CS2 at low–medium settings reaches 80+ fps at 1080p. Valorant comfortably hits 100+ fps. Older AAA games (GTA V, Skyrim, Witcher 3) run at low–medium 1080p. Modern AAA (Cyberpunk, Starfield, Hogwarts Legacy) will struggle. The iGPU is a stop-gap that buys time to save up for a discrete GPU, not a long-term gaming solution.

- **Q: Will I be able to upgrade this PC later?**
  A: AM4 is end-of-life — no new chips coming. You can upgrade to a slightly faster AM4 chip (5700X, 5800X3D if available) but the platform is at its peak. Add a discrete GPU when budget allows; that's the meaningful upgrade for gaming. If you want long-term upgrade path, start on AM5 with the 7500F or higher instead.

- **Q: Do I need a special motherboard?**
  A: Any AM4 motherboard with the right BIOS. B550 is the sweet spot — supports PCIe 4.0 for a future GPU, AGESA microcode for 5000-series. B450 also works after BIOS update. A320 is too old. ExtremePC's services team handles BIOS flashing if your board needs it.

- **Q: What's the cheapest cooler that works?**
  A: An entry-tier budget tower air cooler (ID-COOLING SE-214-XT, Deepcool AG400, or similar) handles 5600G's 65W TDP easily. Don't skip the cooler — there's no stock cooler in OEM packaging.

- **Q: Will it run my 4K monitor for office work?**
  A: Yes, the iGPU outputs 4K 60Hz via HDMI 2.0 or DisplayPort for desktop work, video playback, and light tasks. Just don't expect 4K gaming.

- **Q: Is this OEM "chip-only" or does it come with anything?**
  A: Just the chip in a protective tray. No box, no cooler, no thermal paste tube. Buy a separate cooler (cooler usually includes thermal paste).

- **Q: Stock at Onehunga is only 1 unit — is more coming?**
  A: Stock turnover on AM4 budget chips is regular. If the last one sells, more typically arrive within 1–2 weeks from distributor. Call ExtremePC to check current ETAs.

- **Q: Can I pick up today from Auckland?**
