## FAQ

- **Q: Why no electric lumbar — is this an "old generation" chair?**
  A: No. SE is in current production alongside Omni and Pro. The manual lumbar is a deliberate design choice for users who prefer mechanical reliability over electronic convenience. The screw-dial mechanism is precise (fine-grained adjustment) and has no end-of-life — it lasts as long as the chair frame.

- **Q: Is manual lumbar adjustment annoying day-to-day?**
  A: Only if you change positions frequently. Most users set lumbar once for their spine and leave it. The dial sits behind the lower backrest; one-handed adjustment takes 5 seconds.

- **Q: The base is nylon instead of aluminum — does this matter?**
  A: Nylon base is BIFMA X5.1 certified to the same 100,000 caster cycle standard as the aluminum base. Functionally identical for load and durability. Aluminum on Pro tier is partly aesthetic (premium look) and partly structural reassurance — both materials handle 136kg weight capacity without issue.

Over time, the difference shows up as lower fatigue and less leaning-forward to compensate. If you sit 2–3 hours daily and Secretlab feels fine, you don't need to upgrade.

- **Q: Will the fabric wear out faster at the entry tier?**
