## Comparison
- vs LiberNovo Omni ($2,099): Omni adds electric motorized lumbar for on-the-fly adjustment — worth the $400 upgrade if you frequently change positions throughout the day.
- vs LiberNovo Omni Pro ($2,399): Omni Pro adds electric lumbar, OmniStretch, and active seat fan cooling. If you sit 6+ hours or run warm, the Pro is the better investment.
- vs Secretlab TITAN Evo (~$700–$900 NZD): Secretlab is $800 less and a strong gaming chair. Omni SE's Bionic FlexFit backrest dynamically adapts to movement where Secretlab's lumbar is static — a real ergonomic advantage for back health over time.
- vs Herman Miller Aeron (~$2,800–$4,500+ NZD): Aeron is passive mesh with no foam cushioning. Omni SE's three-zone seat cushion provides better initial comfort. Aeron wins on warranty (12yr) and long-term breathability.
