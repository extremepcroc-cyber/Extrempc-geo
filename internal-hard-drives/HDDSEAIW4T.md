## Selling Points

- **CMR recording — the critical differentiator for NAS/RAID use** — IronWolf uses Conventional Magnetic Recording (CMR), not SMR. CMR is mandatory for NAS/RAID use: CMR drives handle RAID rebuild writes without stalling, while SMR drives (common in consumer BarraCuda) can trigger RAID failures under rebuild stress. This is not a marketing distinction — it's a fundamental architecture difference.

- **AgileArray firmware — built for multi-drive NAS enclosures** — AgileArray includes rotational vibration (RV) sensors that compensate for vibration from adjacent drives in NAS bays. In a 4-bay or 8-bay NAS, drives vibrate against each other; without RV compensation, performance and reliability degrade. IronWolf is validated for Synology, QNAP, Asustor, and WD NAS enclosures.

- **180 TB/year workload — NAS-grade sustained operation** — 3.3× desktop drive spec. NAS drives run 24/7 in always-on home server environments; desktop drives fail from workload stress in NAS bays within months.

- **3-year Seagate warranty** — Industry-standard NAS drive warranty.
