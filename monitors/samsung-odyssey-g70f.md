## FAQ

- **Q: Does Dual Mode actually work at 360Hz — is 1080p on a 4K panel worth it?**
  A: At 1080p/360Hz, the panel runs at 360fps with 360Hz refresh via pixel grouping. The 1080p image on a 4K IPS panel at 27" has some softness due to non-native scaling. At 27" viewing distance (60–80cm), most competitive players find the 360Hz frame-rate advantage outweighs the scaling softness for CS2 and Valorant.

- **Q: Does the G70F support 4K/120Hz on PS5?**
  A: Yes — PS5 outputs 4K/120Hz via HDMI 2.1, which the G70F fully supports.

- **Q: What GPU is needed for 4K/180Hz?**
  A: RTX 5080 can target 4K/120–180fps in less demanding titles at max settings. In demanding titles (Cyberpunk 2077 max settings 4K), even RTX 5080 runs 80–130fps — still within FreeSync Premium Pro range for tear-free output below 180Hz.

- **Q: Is DisplayHDR 600 full-array local dimming?**
  A: Verify on the Samsung product page. DisplayHDR 600 on IPS typically uses edge-lit backlight with limited dimming zones, not FALD. HDR is improved vs DisplayHDR 400 but not equivalent to OLED or FALD. For OLED HDR, look at the Odyssey G80SF series.
