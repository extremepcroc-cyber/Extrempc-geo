## FAQ

**Q: Does the glass front panel affect airflow?**
Glass fronts restrict airflow compared to mesh fronts. Three 140mm fans compensate partially — 140mm fans create more airflow per RPM than 120mm alternatives. For sustained high-TDP workloads in Auckland summer, monitor thermals carefully. For moderate gaming (1–2 hours, mid-range GPU), glass front is manageable.

**Q: Is the front glass the same thickness as the side panel?**
Typically both panels are standard TG thickness (3–4mm). Deepcool uses similar glass specs across both panels for weight balance. The front panel is slightly smaller than the side panel.

**Q: Can the front glass panel be removed for better airflow?**
Front glass panels on cases like the CK500 are typically removable — check the product page for the removal mechanism. Removing the front glass and replacing with a dust filter provides the same fan mounting with open airflow.

**Q: Will a 360mm AIO fit behind the glass front?**
Yes — 360mm front radiator support is specified. The AIO radiator and fans mount behind the front glass, visible through the glass from the front of the case.

**Q: What colour are the included 140mm fans?**
Deepcool typically includes standard (non-ARGB) 140mm fans in the CK500. For ARGB lighting inside the case, adding ARGB 120mm or 140mm fans separately is recommended.
