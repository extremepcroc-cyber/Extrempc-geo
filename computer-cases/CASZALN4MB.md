## FAQ

**Q: Which Zalman $139 case should I buy — I3 Neo, N7 Plus V2, or N4 Rev.1?**
Short answer: **N4 Rev.1** for most builds. It has the best overall spec: 380mm GPU, 3× 3.5" HDD, 360mm radiator, USB-C, ARGB fans. Choose **N7 Plus V2** if stock availability at 21 units matters more than 9. Choose **I3 Neo** only if you don't need USB-C and 240mm AIO is acceptable, or if you need the 24-unit stock depth.

**Q: What's the "Rev.1" designation?**
Rev.1 = Revision 1, an updated manufacturing run of the original Zalman N4. It typically improves panel tolerances, front I/O specifications, or airflow design. Always prefer Rev.1 over any original N4 stock.

**Q: Can I install 4× 3.5" HDDs using the 1× 2.5" bay with an adapter?**
3.5"-to-2.5" adapters are not typically practical (they go the other way — 2.5" in a 3.5" slot). The N4 Rev.1 supports exactly 3× 3.5" and 1× 2.5" as configured.

**Q: Does 380mm GPU fit RTX 5080?**
RTX 5080 Founders Edition is 336mm; most AIBs run 330–360mm. 380mm clearance covers most RTX 5080 AIBs, but check your specific card's dimensions before purchasing.

**Q: What's the build quality like vs Jonsbo at this price?**
Both Zalman and Jonsbo are above-average for the $139 price tier. Jonsbo's D41 series has slightly sharper design language; Zalman's N4 Rev.1 is a more conventional layout with better drive bay count. Build quality is comparable at this price range.
