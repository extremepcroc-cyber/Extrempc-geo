## FAQ

**Q: Is 420mm GPU clearance actually needed?**
Current mainstream GPUs (RTX 5070, RTX 5070 Ti, RX 9070 XT standard AIBs) run 280–340mm — well within 380mm cases. RTX 5080 and RTX 5090 AIBs push 330–400mm on extended triple-fan designs. 420mm is the ceiling specification that removes GPU selection as a concern entirely, including for GPUs not yet released.

**Q: Does the curved top glass panel allow fan mounting?**
The top glass is removable to access the top fan/radiator mounting positions. Remove the top curved panel to install a 360mm top radiator, then the glass sits above the radiator — allowing top glass visibility while maintaining radiator function.

**Q: How do the two USB-C ports differ?**
Check the product page for USB-C specification — typically one USB-C 3.2 Gen1 (5Gbps) for data and one USB-C 3.2 Gen2 (10Gbps) for faster transfer. Two USB-C ports at the front is rare at any case price tier.

**Q: Will the top curved glass scratch easily?**
The top glass panel faces upward and may collect dust — wipe with a soft microfibre cloth only. The convex shape means the top glass surface faces the ceiling, which is less dust-prone than a flat horizontal surface.

**Q: Does the hyperboloid glass require special cleaning products?**
Standard glass cleaner or water + soft microfibre cloth. The curved shape means standard wiping motions follow the curve; no special technique required beyond avoiding abrasive materials.
