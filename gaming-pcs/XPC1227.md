## FAQ

- **Q: Is the Ryzen 7 9700X overkill for gaming?**
  A: No — 8 cores is the sweet spot for most games in 2026. You'll see diminishing returns going to 12 cores unless you're streaming or doing heavy productivity work alongside gaming. In titles like Fortnite and Apex Legends, the 9700X matches the 9900X within 2–3% at 1440p.

- **Q: Can this handle 4K gaming?**
  A: The RTX 5070 can run 4K at medium-high settings, but it's really a 1440p card. For consistent 4K Ultra at 60+ FPS, you'd want the RTX 5080 or higher. That said, with DLSS 4 Quality mode, many titles will hit 60 FPS at 4K — it's just not the card's native strength.

- **Q: Is 1TB storage enough?**
  A: For most gamers, yes — 1TB holds around 15–20 modern AAA titles. If you have a larger library or work with large video files, ExtremePC can upgrade to 2TB at checkout.

- **Q: Does this come with Windows 11?**
  A: Yes, Windows 11 Home is pre-installed and activated.

- **Q: How does the 9700X compare to Intel's i5-14600K?**
  A: The 9700X is more power-efficient (TSMC 4nm vs Intel 7) and runs cooler under sustained loads. Gaming performance is within 3–5% at 1440p. The 9700X wins on efficiency and thermals; the 14600K has a slight edge in some productivity benchmarks.

- **Q: What monitor should I pair with this?**
  A: A 27" 1440p 165Hz IPS panel is the natural pairing. The RTX 5070 will drive this resolution at high settings comfortably. Going to 32" 4K is possible but the GPU will struggle with Ultra settings — 1440p is the sweet spot.
