## FAQ

- **Q: Is the RTX 5070 Ti worth it over the RTX 5070?**
  A: At 1440p, yes — the 5070 Ti delivers 15–20% higher frame rates and has 4GB more VRAM (16GB vs 12GB). The extra VRAM matters in titles like Hogwarts Legacy and The Last of Us. If you want 1440p max settings or 4K headroom, the 5070 Ti is worth the step up.

- **Q: Can this handle 4K gaming?**
  A: The RTX 5070 Ti can run 4K at medium-high settings, but it's really a 1440p card with 4K headroom. For consistent 4K Ultra at 60+ FPS, you'd want the RTX 5080. That said, with DLSS 4 Quality mode, many titles will hit 60 FPS at 4K.

- **Q: Is the Ryzen 7 9700X enough for the RTX 5070 Ti?**
  A: Yes — at 1440p and 4K, the GPU is the limiting factor, not the CPU. In most titles, the 9700X matches the 9900X within 3–5% at these resolutions.

- **Q: Is 1TB storage enough?**

- **Q: Does this come with Windows 11?**

- **Q: What monitor should I pair with this?**
  A: A 27" 1440p 165Hz IPS panel is the natural pairing. The RTX 5070 Ti will drive this resolution at max settings comfortably. A 32" 4K 144Hz panel is also possible — the GPU will handle 4K at medium-high settings.
