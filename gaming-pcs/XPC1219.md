## FAQ

- **Q: Is the Ryzen 7 9700X enough for the RTX 5080?**
  A: At 4K, yes — the GPU is the limiting factor, not the CPU. In most 4K titles, the 9700X matches the 9900X within 2–3%. At 1440p, the 9700X can be slightly bottlenecked by the 5080 in CPU-heavy titles, but the difference is minimal (3–5%).

- **Q: Can this handle 4K gaming at Ultra settings?**
  A: Yes — the RTX 5080 is built for 4K Ultra gaming. In supported titles with DLSS 4, you'll see 80–120 FPS at 4K Ultra. In titles without DLSS support, expect 60–80 FPS at 4K Ultra.

- **Q: Is 1TB storage enough?**

- **Q: Does this come with Windows 11?**

- **Q: What monitor should I pair with this?**
  A: A 27–32" 4K 144Hz+ IPS or OLED panel is the natural pairing. The RTX 5080 will drive 4K at high settings comfortably. Going to 1440p is possible but the GPU will be underutilised — 4K is the sweet spot.

- **Q: How does the RTX 5080 compare to the RTX 4080 Super?**
  A: The RTX 5080 delivers 25–35% higher frame rates at 4K, has more VRAM (16GB vs 16GB but GDDR7 vs GDDR6X), and supports DLSS 4 Multi Frame Generation. It's a meaningful generational upgrade.
