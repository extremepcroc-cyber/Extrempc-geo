## FAQ

- **Q: Single stick vs matched kit — which should I get for a new build?**
  A: For new AM5 / LGA1851 builds: matched 2× 16GB or 2× 32GB kit gives guaranteed dual-channel from the start. Single 32GB stick like this Adata is best for: (a) adding to existing single-stick build for 64GB dual-channel, (b) starting at 32GB single-channel with plan to add another stick later, (c) maximum capacity per slot with future 128GB upgrade headroom.

- **Q: Will single 32GB stick give me dual-channel performance?**
  A: No — a single stick operates in single-channel mode. Gaming performance is ~10–15% lower than dual-channel. For dual-channel, you need a second 32GB stick installed in the matching DIMM slot.

- **Q: Can I mix this with my existing 32GB Adata or different brand?**
  A: Best results: pair two identical Adata AD5U560032G-S sticks. Mixing different brands (even same speed) frequently fails to train at advertised speeds due to silicon variation. Cross-brand mixing risks defaulting to JEDEC slow speed.

- **Q: Will it boot at 5600 MT/s on AM5 / LGA1851?**
  A: Yes — JEDEC standard 5600 MT/s boots automatically without XMP/EXPO enablement. Plug-and-play.

- **Q: Can I overclock to DDR5-6000 for AMD AM5 sweet spot?**
  A: Possibly with manual BIOS tuning — silicon-dependent. Adata doesn't include factory EXPO 6000 profile. For guaranteed AM5 sweet spot 6000 CL30 or 6000 CL36, buy an EXPO-rated kit (Predator PALLAS II, G.SKILL Ripjaws S5).

- **Q: On-die ECC vs full ECC Registered — what's the difference?**
  A: On-die ECC (DDR5 standard, this product) corrects bit errors within the DRAM chip itself. Full ECC Registered (server-grade, like Kingston KSM32RD4/32HDR) also catches errors on the memory bus. For typical desktop use, on-die ECC is sufficient. For scientific / financial workloads needing full ECC, requires server / workstation motherboard.

- **Q: 32GB single stick vs 2× 16GB kit at same total capacity — which?**
  A: 2× 16GB matched kit gives guaranteed dual-channel from boot. Single 32GB stick gives single-channel only, but leaves a slot free for future expansion. For new gaming builds, matched kit. For users who explicitly want one-stick configuration for future flexibility, single 32GB.
