## FAQ

- **Q: Will my laptop support a single 32GB SODIMM stick?**
  A: Most laptops from 2020+ support 32GB single SODIMM. Some older (2018–2019) laptops cap at 16GB per slot regardless of total system capacity claims. Check your laptop spec sheet's "Maximum single-DIMM capacity" or contact ExtremePC's Build Team for confirmation.

- **Q: 32GB single vs 2× 16GB kit — which is better?**
  A: Single 32GB preserves the second slot for future 64GB expansion. 2× 16GB matched kit guarantees dual-channel from boot. For users with future-expansion plans, single 32GB. For maximum dual-channel performance now, 2× 16GB kit.

- **Q: Does single-channel from one stick affect laptop performance?**
  A: Yes — single-channel is ~10–15% slower in some workloads vs dual-channel. For office use, browser, light gaming: marginal. For content creation, AI inference, heavy multitasking: noticeable. Plan to add a second matched stick within reasonable timeframe for full performance.

- **Q: Mixing with existing 16GB stick — safe?**
  A: Mixed-capacity dual-stick configurations (16GB + 32GB) result in 16GB running dual-channel and 16GB running single-channel. Not ideal but works. For optimal performance: same-capacity matched pair.

- **Q: TeamGroup — is the brand reliable in NZ?**
  A: TeamGroup is a Tier 2 mainstream memory brand established since 1997. Solid reputation in NZ enthusiast and OEM markets. Lifetime warranty handled through ExtremePC NZ — straightforward RMA process.

- **Q: Will it boot at full 3200 MT/s on my laptop?**
  A: Yes — JEDEC standard 3200 MT/s. Most DDR4-era laptops (Intel 10th–11th Gen, AMD Ryzen 4000–5000 mobile) officially support DDR4-3200 SODIMM.
