## FAQ

- **Q: Will this work with my AM4 / LGA1200 / LGA1700 motherboard?**
  A: Yes for AM4 (Ryzen 1000–5000), LGA1200 (10th/11th Gen Core), and DDR4 variants of LGA1700 motherboards (Z690 DDR4, B660 DDR4, B760 DDR4). For LGA1700 DDR5 motherboards or AM5 / LGA1851, you need a DDR5 kit instead (not compatible — different notch).

- **Q: I have an existing 16GB stick — can I add this to make 32GB?**
  A: Best results: pair two identical Kingston FURY Beast KF432C16BB1/16 sticks. For mixed kits (different brands or different sticks of same brand), it usually works but may default to slower JEDEC speed instead of XMP 3200 — test with XMP enabled in BIOS.

- **Q: 3200 MT/s vs 3600 MT/s — does the speed difference matter?**
  A: For AM4 Ryzen 5000, 3200 MT/s is the sweet spot because Infinity Fabric (FCLK) runs at 1600 MHz in sync with 3200 RAM. 3600 RAM requires FCLK overclock to 1800 MHz, which not all chips do stably. For LGA1700 Intel DDR4, 3600 has a small advantage. For value-priority builds on AM4, 3200 is the right pick.

- **Q: Is XMP / Plug N Play safe to enable?**
  A: Yes — XMP 2.0 is a stable factory-tested overclock profile. 1.35V is within JEDEC safe range. No long-term reliability impact. For AMD Plug N Play, the auto-overclock to motherboard-max is similarly safe.

- **Q: What's the lifetime warranty process in NZ?**
  A: If a stick fails (rare), contact ExtremePC NZ. ExtremePC processes the warranty domestically — no shipping back to Kingston USA or distributor friction. Replacement is typically issued in 5–10 business days.

- **Q: Will the low-profile heat spreader cool the RAM adequately?**
  A: Yes — DDR4 at 1.35V XMP runs cool even without elaborate heatsinks. The low-profile design ensures cooler clearance with tall air coolers. For sustained heavy workloads (Prime95, AVX-heavy productivity), the heat spreader is sufficient.
