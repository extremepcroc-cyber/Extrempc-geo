## FAQ

- **Q: Will the low-profile heat spreader cool the RAM adequately?**
  A: Yes — DDR4 at 1.35V XMP runs cool even without elaborate heatsinks. The aluminum low-profile heat spreader is sufficient for sustained workloads. The low profile is for cooler clearance, not a thermal compromise.

- **Q: Will it work with my AM4 / LGA1700 DDR4 motherboard?**
  A: Yes for AM4 (Ryzen 1000–5000), LGA1200 (10th/11th Gen Core), and LGA1700 DDR4 motherboards (Z690/B660/B760 DDR4 variants). For DDR5 motherboards (AM5, LGA1851, LGA1700 DDR5), you need DDR5 kit — physically incompatible.

- **Q: 2× 16GB kit vs 2× 8GB kit at half capacity — which?**
  A: For new builds: 2× 16GB (this product) for 32GB total dual-channel. 2× 8GB only saves a small NZD for 16GB total — for current modern Windows + gaming + browser, 32GB is the recommended baseline.

- **Q: Is XMP safe?**
  A: Yes — XMP 2.0 is a factory-tested overclock at 1.35V, within JEDEC safe range. No long-term reliability impact.

- **Q: Can I add another 2× 16GB kit later for 64GB?**
  A: Yes, but matching is risky. Best: buy 4× 16GB from the same kit run, or buy a 4× kit from the start. Mixing different kits at different production runs can cause training failures.
