## FAQ

- **Q: Why CL34 over CL36 at the same 6000 MT/s — does the difference matter?**
  A: ~2–5% gaming improvement in CPU-bound / cache-sensitive titles. For competitive esports (CS2, Valorant) chasing 1% lows, the tighter CL is meaningful. For non-competitive gaming, marginal.

- **Q: AMD EXPO + Intel XMP 3.0 — which profile to enable?**
  A: On AMD AM5 motherboards: enable EXPO. On Intel LGA1700-DDR5 / LGA1851: enable XMP 3.0. The card has both profiles factory-tested. Profile selection is in BIOS.

- **Q: Will RGB sync with my non-Predator motherboard?**
  A: Yes — compatible with ASUS Aura, MSI Mystic Light, Gigabyte RGB Fusion, ASRock Polychrome SYNC. All major motherboard RGB ecosystems supported.

- **Q: Silver aesthetic — what builds does it match?**
  A: White-themed cases (Lian Li O11 Snow, NZXT H7 Flow White, Hyte Y70 White) and silver-accent motherboards (ASUS Prime X670E, ROG Strix B650-A WiFi). For black-themed builds, the Vesta II CL36 RGB Black is the alternative.

- **Q: Can I overclock beyond 6000 CL34?**
  A: Possibly with manual BIOS tuning. Hand-screened ICs are validated for headroom up to 7200 MHz on supported motherboards. Stability is silicon-dependent — start at 6400 CL32 EXPO if available, test stability before pushing higher.

- **Q: AMD AM5 sweet spot — why 6000?**
  A: AMD Ryzen 7000 / 9000 Zen 4 / Zen 5 chips have Infinity Fabric (FCLK) that runs in 1:1 sync with memory speed up to ~2000 MHz. DDR5-6000 keeps FCLK at 2000 in sync — beyond 6000, FCLK desynchs which costs latency. For AM5 builds, 6000 CL30 (or CL34 here) is the sweet spot.

- **Q: Will it fit under my CPU air cooler?**
  A: Vesta II is taller due to the RGB lightbar (~44mm). For tall CPU air coolers (Noctua NH-D15, Phantom Spirit), check RAM clearance specifically. For most 240/280/360mm AIO builds, no issue. For low-profile cooler builds, consider PALLAS II non-RGB variant (lower profile).
