## FAQ

- **Q: Will this work in my laptop?**
  A: Confirm two things first: (1) your laptop uses DDR4 SODIMM (not DDR5, not LPDDR4X-soldered) — most pre-2022 laptops do; (2) the laptop's maximum supported RAM speed is at least DDR4-3200. Most Intel 10th–11th Gen and AMD Ryzen 4000–5000 laptops support DDR4-3200 SODIMM. Check manufacturer documentation or ExtremePC's Build Team for confirmation.

- **Q: 16GB single stick — will it run in dual-channel?**
  A: Only if paired with another 16GB stick in the laptop's second SODIMM slot. Most modern laptops have 2 SODIMM slots; single-stick installation runs in single-channel (~10–15% slower in some benchmarks). For dual-channel, install matched pair.

- **Q: Can I mix Netac with my existing different-brand SODIMM?**
  A: Best results: match brand and speed. Mixing different brands at same speed often works but may default to JEDEC slower speed instead of the listed 3200. For guaranteed full speed, replace both sticks with matched pair.

- **Q: Is the Netac brand reliable for NZ warranty?**
  A: Netac is a newer Chinese value-tier brand. Warranty is handled through ExtremePC NZ — domestic RMA, no overseas shipping friction.

- **Q: Will it work in my old Intel NUC?**
  A: Check NUC model SODIMM compatibility. Most NUC10/NUC11 era models use DDR4 SODIMM. Confirm in NUC spec sheet before purchase.

- **Q: Single 16GB stick today, can I add another later?**
  A: Yes — best to add an identical Netac NTBSD4N32SP-16 for guaranteed compatibility. Mixing brands later may force single-channel or JEDEC slow default.
