## FAQ

- **Q: Will this work in my laptop?**
  A: Confirm two things: (1) your laptop uses DDR5 SODIMM (not soldered LPDDR5/LPDDR5X, not DDR4) — most 2023+ laptops do; (2) the laptop supports DDR5-5600 (current Intel 13th/14th Gen, Intel Core Ultra, AMD Ryzen 7000+ mobile all support 5600). Some ultrathin laptops use soldered memory with no upgrade option.

- **Q: ADATA vs HP-qualified at same NZD — which?**
  A: For corporate HP procurement compliance, HP-qualified. For personal HP upgrades or non-HP laptops, ADATA is equivalent in performance with Tier 2 brand recognition. The HP premium is policy-driven, not performance.

- **Q: ADATA vs Whalekom — Tier 2 vs value-tier brand difference?**
  A: ADATA has 20+ years global memory manufacturing reputation and broader NZ retail recognition. Whalekom is value-tier brand newer to NZ at lower NZD. For brand-conscious buyers or longer-warranty-tested validation, ADATA. For absolute value with same JEDEC compliance, Whalekom.

- **Q: 16GB single stick — will it run in dual-channel?**
  A: Only if paired with another 16GB stick in the laptop's second SODIMM slot. Single-stick runs in single-channel (~10–15% slower in some benchmarks). For dual-channel, install matched pair (preferably 2× ADATA AD5S560016G-S for guaranteed match).

- **Q: Can I mix ADATA with my existing different-brand DDR5 SODIMM?**
  A: Best results: match brand and speed. Mixing different DDR5 brands frequently fails to train at full speed. For dual-channel reliability, replace both sticks with matched pair.
