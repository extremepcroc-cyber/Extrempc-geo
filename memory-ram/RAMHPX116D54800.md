## FAQ

- **Q: My HP G9 / G10 — does it actually use DDR5-4800 or DDR5-5600?**
  A: Generally: HP G9 and early G10 (2022 / early 2023) = DDR5-4800. Later G10 (mid-2023+) and G11 / G1i = DDR5-5600. Check the specific HP model spec sheet or consult ExtremePC's Build Team. For HP G9 specifically (Intel 12th Gen Alder Lake-P), 4800 is the answer.

- **Q: Will DDR5-5600 work on my HP G9 instead?**
  A: Yes — DDR5-5600 SODIMM auto-downclocks to 4800 on G9 platforms. But it's not HP-qualified at that speed, may trigger BIOS unqualified-memory warnings, and won't run at its higher rated speed (no benefit). For HP G9 / early G10 specifically, the DDR5-4800 HP-qualified part is the right pick.

- **Q: HP G10 — DDR5-4800 or DDR5-5600?**
  A: Early G10 production runs use DDR5-4800; later G10 production switched to DDR5-5600 as Intel platform improved. Check your specific laptop's BIOS or service manual. The DDR5-4800 (this product) is qualified for early G10; the DDR5-5600 HP variant is qualified for later G10 / G11 / G1i.

- **Q: HP-qualified vs generic — when does it actually matter?**
  A: For corporate HP support contracts, audit compliance, or schools/government procurement — yes, HP-qualified is the policy answer. For personal upgrades on HP G9/G10 outside corporate support, generic 5600 (downclocked to 4800) works equivalently.
