## FAQ

- **Q: SODIMM vs UDIMM — what's the practical difference?**
  A: Physical size and pin count. **SODIMM** (260/262-pin DDR4/DDR5) is compact laptop format (67.6mm length). **UDIMM** (288-pin DDR4/DDR5) is full-length desktop format (133mm length). They use different physical slots and are not interchangeable. Installing the wrong form factor either doesn't fit or damages the system.

- **Q: Why is UDIMM listed in the Laptop RAM category?**
  A: Some HP small form factor systems (HP X2 line, HP Mini desktop, HP Z Mini Workstation) use UDIMM internally despite being marketed alongside laptops. ExtremePC categorizes by HP's product line organization. The "Laptop RAM" category here covers HP X2 / mini-PC platforms that share retail merchandising space with laptops.

- **Q: Will this fit my regular HP laptop (Probook / Elitebook)?**
  A: **No.** Regular HP laptops (Probook, Elitebook, Zbook) use SODIMM, not UDIMM. This part won't physically fit those laptops. For HP Probook / Elitebook / Zbook G10/G11/G1i DDR5-5600 upgrades, use RAMHPX116D55600 (the SODIMM SKU).

- **Q: How do I know if my HP system uses UDIMM or SODIMM?**
  A: Check HP's official spec sheet for your specific model, or contact ExtremePC's Build Team with your HP system model number. Build Team will confirm form factor before purchase to prevent a wrong-format order.

- **Q: HP-qualified vs generic UDIMM at lower NZD — does qualification matter?**
  A: For corporate HP support contracts and procurement compliance — yes. For personal upgrades on HP X2 / mini-PC without corporate support, generic Adata / Kingston / G.SKILL desktop UDIMM at lower NZD is functionally equivalent.

- **Q: Can I use this in my regular desktop PC?**
  A: Yes physically — it's a standard 288-pin DDR5 UDIMM. Compatible with AMD AM5 / Intel LGA1700-DDR5 / LGA1851 desktop motherboards. But cheaper desktop DDR5 UDIMM alternatives exist (Netac, Adata, Whalekom) for non-HP desktop builds.
