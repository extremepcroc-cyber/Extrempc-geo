## FAQ

- **Q: No XMP / EXPO — will this work on AM5 / LGA1851 motherboards?**
  A: Yes — boots at JEDEC standard 5600 MT/s automatically without any BIOS configuration. No XMP/EXPO needed. For users who want plug-and-play DDR5 without fiddling, this is actually a benefit.

- **Q: 5600 MT/s vs 6000 MT/s on AMD — does the speed difference matter?**
  A: On AMD Zen 4 (Ryzen 7000) and Zen 5 (Ryzen 9000) chips, the Infinity Fabric sweet spot is DDR5-6000 with FCLK at 2000 MHz. Running at 5600 keeps FCLK at ~1867 MHz — slightly less ideal sync. Real-world gaming difference: ~3–5% in CPU-bound titles. For absolute budget builds, acceptable trade-off; for performance-priority AM5 builds, step up to 6000 CL30 EXPO.

- **Q: 5600 MT/s on Intel — is this fine?**
  A: Yes. Intel 12th/13th/14th Gen and Core Ultra 200 series all officially support DDR5-5600 (and higher). For Intel builds, the 5600 baseline is fully utilized.

- **Q: CL46 latency — how does that compare?**
  A: CL46 at 5600 MT/s is the JEDEC standard for value-tier DDR5. Premium kits (5600 CL36 or 6000 CL30) have noticeably lower effective latency. Real-world gaming difference: 2–5% in some titles. For non-competitive gaming and productivity, not meaningful; for esports / competitive gaming chasing 1% lows, premium kits matter more.

- **Q: On-die ECC — is this server-grade ECC?**
  A: No. On-die ECC corrects bit errors within the DRAM chip itself, but doesn't extend to errors during transit on the memory bus (which true server ECC catches). On-die ECC is a meaningful reliability improvement over DDR4 but not equivalent to full server ECC. For typical desktop use, more than sufficient.

- **Q: Can I mix this with other DDR5 brands for dual-channel?**
  A: Strongly recommended not to. Mixing different DDR5 sticks (even same speed/capacity) frequently fails to train at advertised speeds due to silicon variation across manufacturers. For dual-channel, buy a matched 2× 16GB kit instead.

- **Q: Heatsink-less — will it overheat?**
  A: At 1.1V JEDEC operation, DDR5 doesn't generate enough heat to require a heatsink for normal use. Heatsinks matter for overclocked / high-frequency kits (6400+ MT/s) and aesthetic showcase builds. For 5600 MT/s JEDEC operation, no concern.
