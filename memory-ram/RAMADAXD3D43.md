## FAQ

- **Q: 32GB vs 16GB — do I need 32GB?**
  A: For 1080p / 1440p gaming alone: 16GB sufficient. For gaming + streaming, content creation, browser with many tabs, virtualization, or any AI workload: 32GB recommended. For users targeting 5-year build longevity, 32GB future-proofs.

- **Q: Will this work with my AM4 / LGA1200 / LGA1700 motherboard?**
  A: Yes for AM4 (Ryzen 1000–5000 with appropriate BIOS), LGA1200 (10th/11th Gen Core), and LGA1700 DDR4 motherboards (Z690 DDR4, B660 DDR4, B760 DDR4 variants). For LGA1700 DDR5 motherboards, AM5, or LGA1851, you need DDR5 instead (different notch — physically incompatible).

- **Q: 2× 16GB vs 4× 8GB at same capacity — which is better?**
  A: 2× 16GB is the sweet spot. Most consumer motherboards have 4 DIMM slots; populating 2 gives dual-channel and leaves 2 slots for future upgrade to 64GB. Populating all 4 with 8GB sticks risks reduced max speed (some Ryzen platforms can't sustain XMP 3200 with 4 DIMMs).

- **Q: Is XMP safe to enable?**
  A: Yes — XMP 2.0 is factory-tested overclock at 1.35V. Within JEDEC safe range. No reliability impact for daily use.

- **Q: 3200 MT/s vs 3600 MT/s — does the speed matter for AM4?**
  A: For Ryzen 5000, 3200 MT/s is the Infinity Fabric sweet spot (FCLK at 1600 MHz in sync). 3600 requires FCLK overclock that not all chips do stably. For value-tier AM4 builds, 3200 is the right pick.

- **Q: Lifetime warranty — how does it work in NZ?**
  A: If a stick fails (rare), contact ExtremePC NZ. RMA is processed domestically — no overseas shipping back to ADATA HQ. Replacement issued in 5–10 business days.

- **Q: Will the heatsink fit under my CPU air cooler?**
  A: XPG Gammix D35 is moderately tall (~42mm). For most CPU air coolers (Thermalright Peerless Assassin 120, ID-COOLING SE-224-XTS), fits with no issue. For very tight builds with downdraft coolers or top-fin clearance issues, the lower-profile PNY XLR8 alternative may be better.
