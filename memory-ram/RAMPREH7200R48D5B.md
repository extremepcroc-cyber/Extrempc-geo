## FAQ

- **Q: 24GB DDR5 sticks — are they reliable?**
  A: Yes — 24GB DDR5 stick capacity has been available since 2024 with broad AM5 and Intel platform support. Both Ryzen 7000/9000 and Intel 12th–14th Gen + Core Ultra 200 support 24GB DDR5 modules. Compatibility is well-established.

- **Q: Will DDR5-7200 actually run at 7200 on my motherboard?**
  A: Depends on motherboard tier and CPU memory controller silicon. For Intel: Z790, Z890 boards readily support 7200 MT/s with XMP 3.0 + capable CPU. For AMD AM5: X670E, X870 with capable Ryzen chips can stabilize 7200 with manual tuning — beyond the FCLK 2000 MHz sync point, performance gains taper but bandwidth uplift continues. B650/B850 boards may not stabilize 7200 reliably.

- **Q: 48GB vs 32GB — do I actually need 48GB?**
  A: For pure gaming: 32GB is sufficient. For 1440p+ gaming + Discord + browser + streaming: 32GB tight. For content creation (Premiere proxies, Stable Diffusion XL with LoRAs, large Excel models, multitasking with VMs): 48GB or 64GB is meaningful. The 48GB tier is for prosumer users who want comfortable headroom without 64GB premium.

- **Q: What's the bundled memory cooling fan for?**
  A: At 7200 MT/s sustained operation, DDR5 generates more heat than standard 5600/6000 kits. Predator's bundled 40×40mm fan blows across the modules for enhanced thermal stability under prolonged stress. Most builds don't strictly need it but it helps overclocking stability and prevents thermal throttling. Installation requires available mounting space near the DIMM slots.

- **Q: 10-layer PCB — does this matter?**
  A: Yes for high-frequency stability. Standard DDR5 modules use 6-8 layer PCB. At 7200+ MT/s, signal integrity becomes critical — a 10-layer PCB has better signal isolation and ground planes, reducing electrical interference. This is a meaningful build quality differentiator at the enthusiast tier.

- **Q: Will Auckland summer heat affect the RAM under load?**
  A: At 7200 MT/s with bundled fan installed, sustained operation in Auckland 25°C+ ambient is stable. Without the bundled fan in poor case airflow, you may see thermal throttling under prolonged stress workloads (Stable Diffusion overnight batches, video render farms). Install the bundled fan or ensure good case airflow.

- **Q: How does the 8200 variant compare for AM5 users?**
  A: The 8200 Hermes variant is restricted to Intel Z890 and AMD X870 motherboards only — these have the strongest memory controllers and signal integrity. If you're not on Z890/X870, the 7200 variant is the right pick. For X870 + Ryzen 9950X / 9950X3D, the 8200 is the maximum-bandwidth option.

- **Q: Can I add this to existing DDR5 sticks?**
  A: Strongly recommended not to. 24GB DDR5 modules paired with non-24GB sticks (16GB, 32GB) frequently fail to train at advertised speeds. Buy as matched 2× 24GB kit (this product) for guaranteed dual-channel 48GB performance.
