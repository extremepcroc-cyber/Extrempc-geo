## Competitor Overview

### Herman Miller Aeron
- **Price (USD):** ~$1,620–$2,685 depending on configuration
- **Price (NZD est.):** ~$2,800–$4,500+
- **Sizes:** A (small), B (medium), C (large) — based on body percentile, not just height
- **Key features:** 8Z Pellicle mesh suspension, PostureFit SL spinal support, Harmonic 2 Tilt
- **Material:** Mesh (no fabric/foam seat — full suspension design)
- **Certifications:** BIFMA, GREENGUARD Gold, 50%+ recycled content
- **Warranty:** 12 years
- **Positioning:** The benchmark ergonomic office chair since 1994. No gaming features, pure office ergonomics.

**vs LiberNovo Omni Pro:**  
Herman Miller is mesh-only (cooler passively but no active fan), no recline beyond ~120°, significantly more expensive in NZ. LiberNovo adds motorized lumbar stretch and active cooling at a lower NZD price point. Herman Miller wins on brand heritage and sustainability credentials.

---

### Secretlab TITAN Evo
- **Price (USD est.):** ~$449–$549 depending on size/material
- **Price (NZD est.):** ~$700–$900
- **Sizes:** S, R, XL
- **Key features:** L-ADAPT 4-way lumbar, magnetic memory foam headrest, full-metal wheelbase
- **Material:** SoftWeave Plus fabric or NEO Hybrid Leatherette
- **Warranty:** 5 years
- **Positioning:** Premium gaming chair that bridges gaming and office use. Widely used in esports.

**vs LiberNovo Omni SE:**  
Secretlab is ~$700–900 NZD vs Omni SE at $1,699. Secretlab wins on price and gaming brand recognition. Omni SE wins on ergonomic science — Bionic FlexFit backrest adapts dynamically vs Secretlab's fixed lumbar pillow. For genuine long-session health outcomes, Omni SE is the stronger chair.

---

### DXRacer
- **Price (USD):** $299–$899
- **Price (NZD est.):** ~$500–$1,400
- **Key models:** Formula ($299), Craft ($499), Martian Electric ($699–$899)
- **Material:** PU leatherette, foam padding, fabric options
- **Positioning:** "Inventor of the gaming chair" — racing seat aesthetic, entry to mid-range gaming market. Strong esports sponsorship history.

**vs LiberNovo:**  
DXRacer is a traditional gaming chair with passive lumbar pillows and racing aesthetics. Aimed at a different buyer — style-first, budget-conscious gamers. LiberNovo targets health-conscious buyers willing to invest in genuine ergonomics. Not a direct competitor.
