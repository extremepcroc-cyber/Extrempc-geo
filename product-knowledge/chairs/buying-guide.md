## Gaming Chair vs Ergonomic Chair — What's the Difference?

| | Gaming Chair | Ergonomic Chair |
|---|---|---|
| Design origin | Racing seat aesthetic | Office/health science |
| Lumbar support | Detachable pillow (passive) | Built-in adjustable (active) |
| Recline | Often deep (135°+) | Functional range (100°–135°) |
| Materials | Mostly PU leather / leatherette | Mesh, high-grade fabric |
| Long-session comfort | Variable | Purpose-built |
| Price range | $300–$900 NZD | $700–$3,500+ NZD |

## How to Choose by Seat Depth

Seat depth directly affects thigh pressure and circulation. Getting this wrong causes discomfort within 1–2 hours.

| Seat Depth | Best For | Height Guide |
|---|---|---|
| **45cm** | Shorter legs, petite frame | ~153–172cm |
| **48cm** | Longer legs, taller frame | ~170–186cm |

**Rule of thumb:** sitting fully back in the chair, there should be a 2–3 finger gap between the seat edge and the back of the knee. If the seat digs into the back of the knee, go shorter. If legs feel unsupported, go deeper.

## Key Ergonomic Features Explained

**Bionic FlexFit Backrest** (all LiberNovo Omni)  
8 flexible panels with 16 pivot points that flex with your movement rather than forcing a fixed posture. Unlike rigid backrests, this adapts to micro-movements throughout the day.

**OmniStretch Motorized Lumbar** (Omni Pro only)  
Electric lumbar that can actively stretch and decompress the lower spine — useful after long sitting periods. Similar in concept to lumbar massage but targeted at the L4–L5 vertebrae zone.

**Active Airflow Ventilation** (Omni Pro only)  
Centrifugal fan (4000 RPM) built into the seat cushion. Pulls heat and moisture away from the body. Particularly relevant in NZ summer or for users who run warm.

**SyncroLink Dynamic System** (all Omni)  
60+ interconnected joints linking headrest, backrest, armrests, and seat. As you shift weight, all components adjust together — prevents the "fighting the chair" feeling of rigid designs.
