# Jonsbo ITX / NAS Case Knowledge Base

## Brand Overview

Jonsbo is a Chinese PC case manufacturer founded in 2008, known for compact ITX and NAS-focused enclosures at aggressive price points. Strong presence in the DIY NAS community globally. Distributed exclusively in NZ through ExtremePC.

## Product Line — NAS Series (N-series)

### Jonsbo N2
- **Form factor:** ITX only
- **Drive bays:** 5× 3.5" HDD + 1× 2.5" SSD (front-loading tool-free trays)
- **Expansion:** 1× PCIe slot (half-height)
- **CPU cooler height:** max 56mm (low-profile only)
- **PSU:** SFX / SFX-L
- **Dimensions:** 210 × 242 × 230mm (~12L)
- **Material:** Steel + mesh panels
- **Colors:** Black, White
- **Target:** Home NAS / media server builds, budget-conscious

### Jonsbo N3
- **Form factor:** ITX only
- **Drive bays:** 8× 3.5" HDD + 2× 2.5" SSD (tool-free trays)
- **Expansion:** 1× PCIe slot (half-height)
- **CPU cooler height:** max 56mm (low-profile only)
- **PSU:** SFX / SFX-L
- **Dimensions:** 210 × 290 × 253mm (~15L)
- **Material:** Steel + mesh front
- **Colors:** Black
- **Target:** Power users needing 8-bay NAS in compact footprint — Plex, TrueNAS, HomeAssistant

### Jonsbo N4
- **Form factor:** ITX and M-ATX (dual compatibility)
- **Drive bays:** 4× 3.5" HDD + 2× 2.5" SSD (tool-free trays)
- **Expansion:** 2× PCIe slots (full-height when M-ATX)
- **CPU cooler height:** max 82mm
- **PSU:** ATX supported (M-ATX config), SFX in ITX config
- **Dimensions:** 235 × 210 × 330mm (~16L)
- **Material:** Steel mesh
- **Colors:** Black, White
- **Target:** Hybrid NAS + light gaming rig, M-ATX flexibility, taller cooler support

## Key Differentiators vs Competitors

### NAS Cases (N-series vs Fractal Node, Silverstone CS series)
- Jonsbo N-series: lower price point with tool-free HDD trays, more bays per dollar
- Fractal Node 804: dual-chamber, better cable management, higher price tier
- Silverstone CS380: rack-adjacent, more enterprise-feel, higher NZD
- **Jonsbo advantage:** best bay-count per NZD in NZ market, ExtremePC exclusive

### SFF Cases (T9 vs NCase M1, Lian Li A4)
- T9: full aluminum at entry-to-mid SFF price — usually aluminum SFF cases cost significantly more
- NCase M1: better known brand, higher price, better community support, similar volume
- Lian Li A4-H2O: optimized for 240mm AIO, different thermal approach
- **T9 advantage:** full aluminum construction at more accessible NZD price point, clean aesthetic

## FAQ Prep

**Q: Can I use a full-size ATX PSU in the N-series?**
N2/N3: No — SFX/SFX-L only. N4 in M-ATX config: ATX supported.

**Q: What's the max GPU length in the N-series?**
N-series are NAS cases — GPU support is minimal (half-height slot). Not intended for gaming GPUs. T9 supports up to 200mm GPU.

**Q: Can I use a 120mm AIO in the N3?**
Not officially supported — top panel has no radiator mount. Air cooling only (max 56mm).

**Q: Hot-swap HDDs?**
Tool-free tray removal, but not true hot-swap — requires OS-level hot-plug support and appropriate HBA/controller.

**Q: Warranty?**
1-year NZ warranty through ExtremePC Onehunga.
