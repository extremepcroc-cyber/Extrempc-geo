# Mid Tower Cases — Jonsbo & Segotep Knowledge Base

## Jonsbo Mid Tower Overview

Jonsbo is a Chinese case manufacturer known in the enthusiast market for distinctive design language, above-average build quality for the price tier, and unique aesthetic choices (wood accents, hyperboloid glass, Sea View panoramic glass). Their mid-tower lineup at ExtremePC spans from the entry D41 series to the premium BO400 aluminum frame.

---

## Jonsbo D41 Series

Two variants: D41 STD (standard TG) and D41 Mesh (mesh front panel).

### D41 STD Black — CASJOND41STDB — $149

- **Form factor:** ATX mid-tower
- **Side panel:** Tempered glass
- **Front panel:** Solid / vented steel — not mesh
- **CPU cooler clearance:** up to 185mm
- **GPU length:** up to 400mm
- **Radiator support:** 360mm front, 240mm top
- **Drive bays:** 2× 3.5" + 2× 2.5"
- **Front I/O:** 2× USB 3.0, 1× USB-C, HD Audio
- **Included fans:** none
- **PCIe slots:** 7
- **Dimensions:** ~450 × 215 × 470mm
- **Stock OH:** 13
- **Key differentiator:** 400mm GPU clearance and 360mm front radiator at an entry-level ATX price ($149). Most ATX cases at $149 compromise on GPU length or front radiator size.

### D41 Mesh Black — CASJOND41MEB — $119

- **Form factor:** ATX mid-tower
- **Side panel:** Tempered glass
- **Front panel:** Mesh — optimised for airflow
- **CPU cooler clearance:** up to 185mm
- **GPU length:** up to 400mm
- **Radiator support:** 360mm front, 240mm top
- **Drive bays:** 2× 3.5" + 2× 2.5"
- **Front I/O:** 2× USB 3.0, 1× USB-C, HD Audio
- **Included fans:** none
- **Stock OH:** 5
- **Key differentiator:** Mesh front panel at $119 — $30 cheaper than D41 STD. Mesh = better airflow (GPU/CPU temp typically 2–5°C lower in equivalent fan configurations vs solid panels). The go-to recommendation for performance-first ATX builds at the entry price.
- **D41 STD vs D41 Mesh:** STD has cleaner look (solid front); Mesh has better thermals. Same internal specs, same 400mm GPU clearance, same 360mm radiator support. Aesthetic vs airflow tradeoff at $30 price difference.

## Jonsbo TK-2 — CASJONTK2B — $229

- **Form factor:** ATX mid-tower
- **Glass type:** Hyperboloid tempered glass — curved convex side panel (not flat TG)
- **Colour:** Black
- **CPU cooler clearance:** up to 170mm
- **GPU length:** up to 380mm
- **Radiator support:** 360mm front, 240mm top
- **Drive bays:** 2× 3.5" + 2× 2.5"
- **Front I/O:** 2× USB 3.0, 1× USB-C, HD Audio
- **Stock OH:** 2
- **Key differentiator:** Hyperboloid (curved) glass side panel — the glass bows outward from the case in a convex curve rather than sitting flat. This creates a distinctive 3D visual effect: components inside appear to float behind a curved glass lens. Jonsbo is one of very few brands producing hyperboloid glass cases; this design language is unique in the NZ market. Premium appearance at $229 that flat-panel TG cases can't replicate.
- **NZ context:** Limited to 2 units — contact ExtremePC to confirm. The TK-2's hyperboloid glass is a deliberate design purchase; buyers specifically seek it out.

## Jonsbo TK-4 — CASJONTK4B — $329

- **Form factor:** ATX mid-tower
- **Glass type:** Hyperbolic Sea View Room glass — curved glass covering BOTH side AND top panels
- **Colour:** Black
- **CPU cooler clearance:** up to 185mm
- **GPU length:** up to 420mm
- **Radiator support:** 360mm front, 280/360mm top
- **Drive bays:** 2× 3.5" + 4× 2.5"
- **Front I/O:** 2× USB 3.0, 2× USB-C, HD Audio
- **Included fans:** typically 3× ARGB fans
- **Stock OH:** 19 — best-stocked premium case at ExtremePC
- **Key differentiator:** Combines Jonsbo's hyperboloid glass with the Sea View Room dual-angle visibility. The side glass is curved; the top glass is also curved/panoramic — both side and top interior are visible simultaneously through curved glass. 420mm GPU clearance is the highest of any case in the ExtremePC Mid Tower range. This is the flagship Jonsbo showcase ATX case.
- **TK-4 vs TK-2:** TK-4 adds top glass panel, 420mm vs 380mm GPU, more USB-C ports, for $100 more. TK-4 is the definitive version; TK-2 is for buyers who want the hyperboloid glass aesthetic at a lower price.

## Jonsbo BO400CG — CASJONBO400G — $579

- **Form factor:** ATX mid-tower
- **Construction:** Aluminum frame with tempered glass panels — CNC-machined aluminum extrusions
- **Colour:** Gray (CG = Chrome Gray or similar aluminum anodised finish)
- **CPU cooler clearance:** up to 170mm
- **GPU length:** up to 380mm
- **Radiator support:** 360mm front, 240mm top
- **Front I/O:** 2× USB 3.0, 1× USB-C, HD Audio
- **Stock OH:** 3
- **Key differentiator:** Aluminum frame construction — the BO400 is not a steel case with aluminum accents; the primary structural frame is extruded aluminum with glass panel inserts. At $579, this is the premium workstation/enthusiast case tier. The aluminum construction gives significantly lower weight per rigidity ratio vs steel, premium material feel, and a distinct visual identity. In NZ, aluminum-frame ATX cases at this size are uncommon at local stock.
- **Target buyer:** Enthusiast/professional who treats the case as a long-term investment — would own for 5–10 years across multiple hardware generations. The BO400 is a statement case, not a budget decision.

## Segotep Mid Tower Models

### Segotep LUX 360 White — CASSEGLUX360W — $89

- **Form factor:** ATX mid-tower
- **Side panel:** Acrylic (not tempered glass) — lower cost material, lighter, more scratch-prone than TG
- **Colour:** White
- **Key feature:** Named "LUX 360" — 360mm radiator support is the headline spec. Front 360mm radiator mount at $89 is the primary value proposition.
- **CPU cooler clearance:** up to 165mm
- **GPU length:** up to 330mm
- **Drive bays:** 2× 3.5" + 2× 2.5"
- **Front I/O:** 2× USB 3.0, HD Audio (check for USB-C)
- **Stock OH:** 6
- **Key differentiator:** $89 white ATX case with 360mm front radiator support. The acrylic panel is the trade-off vs TG alternatives — acrylic scratches more easily but is lighter and cheaper to produce. For builds where budget is the primary constraint and AIO liquid cooling is planned, LUX 360 White delivers the radiator clearance without the TG premium.
- **Positioning:** The cheapest entry to white ATX at ExtremePC. Below Segotep Endura Pro+ White at $115 and Zalman entry models at $139.

### Segotep Endura Pro+ EATX White — CASSEGEPPW — $115

- **Form factor:** EATX / Full Tower (not strictly mid-tower despite category placement)
- **Board support:** EATX (Extended ATX, up to 305×330mm boards) — broader than standard ATX
- **Side panel:** Tempered glass
- **Colour:** White
- **CPU cooler clearance:** up to 180mm
- **GPU length:** up to 400mm
- **Radiator support:** 360mm front, 360mm top
- **Drive bays:** 3× 3.5" + 4× 2.5"
- **Front I/O:** 2× USB 3.0, 1× USB-C, HD Audio
- **Stock OH:** 7
- **Key differentiator:** EATX support at $115 is exceptional value. EATX motherboards (used in high-end X670E workstations, HEDT platforms like Threadripper) require cases that fit up to 305×330mm boards — most $115 cases don't support EATX. For builders with EATX boards, or planning to step up to EATX in future, the Endura Pro+ White at $115 is a significant value outlier. Also supports 400mm GPU and 360mm top + front radiators.
- **Note:** "Full Tower" category nomenclature — it's listed in mid-tower BC category but physically/spec-wise is a full/EATX tower.
