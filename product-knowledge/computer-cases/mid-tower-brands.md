## Corsair 3000D Airflow RGB — CASCOR3000DAFRW — $229

- **Form factor:** ATX mid-tower
- **Front panel:** Mesh airflow front panel (Airflow series = high-airflow mesh)
- **Side panel:** Tempered glass
- **Colour:** White
- **CPU cooler clearance:** up to 170mm
- **GPU length:** up to 360mm
- **Radiator support:** 360mm front, 240mm top
- **Drive bays:** 2× 3.5" + 2× 2.5"
- **Front I/O:** 2× USB 3.0, 1× USB-C 3.1, HD Audio
- **Included fans:** 3× Corsair SP120 RGB fans (120mm, 3-pin PWM, addressable RGB)
- **RGB control:** Corsair iCUE compatible — full software control via Corsair's ecosystem
- **Stock OH:** 1 (very limited)
- **Brand context:** Corsair is an established US gaming hardware brand (20+ years), dominant in NZ for PSUs, RAM, and peripherals. The 3000D is their mainstream mid-tower line; the "Airflow" variant is the mesh-front version optimised for thermals vs the standard 3000D which has a solid front.
- **Key differentiator:** Corsair brand name recognition — builders who already own Corsair PSU, RAM, AIO, or peripherals often choose Corsair cases for ecosystem consistency. The iCUE software integration means all Corsair ARGB components (fans, AIO pump, RAM) synchronise in one app. At $229, it competes with Jonsbo TK-2 ($229) but offers brand recognition and iCUE ecosystem over Jonsbo's hyperboloid glass aesthetic.
- **Limitation:** 1 unit only — contact ExtremePC for restock timing. Not a high-volume stocking item.

## Deepcool CK500 — CASDEECK500B — $179

- **Form factor:** ATX mid-tower
- **Front panel:** Dual-sided tempered glass (glass front + glass side)
- **Side panel:** Tempered glass
- **Colour:** Black
- **CPU cooler clearance:** up to 175mm
- **GPU length:** up to 380mm
- **Radiator support:** 360mm front, 240mm top
- **Drive bays:** 2× 3.5" + 4× 2.5"
- **Front I/O:** 2× USB 3.0, 1× USB-C 3.2 Gen2 (10Gbps), HD Audio
- **Included fans:** 3× 140mm fans (non-ARGB typically — verify with product page)
- **Stock OH:** 3
- **Brand context:** Deepcool is a Chinese cooling and case manufacturer with growing international presence. Known for AIO coolers (Deepcool LE/LS/LT series), air coolers (AK400, AK620), and cases (CH/CK series). Deepcool cases punch above their price point in airflow and build quality.
- **Key differentiator:** Glass front panel — the CK500 is distinguished by having a full-height tempered glass front panel in addition to the standard glass side panel. This creates a "two-sided glass" showcase effect unique in this price range — the front of the case shows the AIO radiator and front fans from outside, not just through the side. Also note: 4× 2.5" bays is above average for this tier. USB-C at 10Gbps (Gen2) is faster than most competitors at $179 who offer Gen1 (5Gbps).
- **Airflow note:** Glass front panels reduce airflow vs mesh fronts. The CK500 trades thermal performance for aesthetics. Suitable for builds with moderate TDP in Auckland's climate (25°C ambient summer); high-TDP builds (Core i9, RTX 5080) may prefer a mesh-front case.

## Zalman Series (N4, N7 Plus V2, I3 Neo)

Zalman is a South Korean brand (founded 1999) with long heritage in cooling and cases. In NZ via ExtremePC, Zalman occupies the value-entry ATX mid-tower segment ($139) with three models that differ in features.

### Zalman I3 Neo Black — CASZALI3NEOB — $139

- **Form factor:** ATX mid-tower
- **Side panel:** Tempered glass (full-height)
- **Colour:** Black
- **CPU cooler clearance:** up to 165mm
- **GPU length:** up to 350mm
- **Radiator support:** 240mm front, 240mm top
- **Drive bays:** 2× 3.5" + 3× 2.5"
- **Front I/O:** 2× USB 3.0, HD Audio (no USB-C)
- **Included fans:** 3× 120mm ARGB fans (front × 2, rear × 1) + RGB controller
- **Stock OH:** 24 — highest-stocked individual case in the Mid Tower range
- **Key differentiator:** 3× ARGB fans included at $139 — the best "total included value" in the entry ATX tier. The RGB controller means ARGB fans work even without a 5V ARGB header on the motherboard (useful on budget B550/H610 boards with no ARGB headers). 24 units in Onehunga = the most available mid-tower case at ExtremePC. The I3 Neo is the volume seller.
- **Trade-off:** No USB-C front I/O (USB 3.0 only). 240mm radiator limit (not 360mm). For entry builds, these are acceptable limitations; for premium builds, step up to Zalman N7 Plus V2 or higher.

### Zalman N7 Plus V2 — CASZALN7PV2 — $139

- **Form factor:** ATX mid-tower
- **Side panel:** Tempered glass
- **Colour:** Black (with mesh/vented front panel design)
- **CPU cooler clearance:** up to 165mm
- **GPU length:** up to 350mm
- **Radiator support:** 360mm front, 240mm top
- **Drive bays:** 2× 3.5" + 3× 2.5"
- **Front I/O:** 2× USB 3.0, 1× USB-C, HD Audio
- **Included fans:** 3× 120mm ARGB fans + RGB hub
- **Stock OH:** 21
- **Key differentiator vs I3 Neo (same price $139):** N7 Plus V2 adds USB-C and 360mm front radiator support at the same $139 price. For AIO builds with a 360mm front radiator, N7 Plus V2 is the right choice over I3 Neo. For builds without AIO, I3 Neo and N7 Plus V2 are comparable — N7 Plus V2 adds USB-C for the same money.
- **Summary I3 Neo vs N7 Plus V2:** Same price, N7 Plus V2 wins on radiator size (360mm vs 240mm) and USB-C. I3 Neo has identical fan count. At $139, N7 Plus V2 is the better spec for most builds.

### Zalman N4 Rev.1 Black — CASZALN4MB — $139

- **Form factor:** ATX mid-tower
- **Side panel:** Tempered glass
- **Colour:** Black
- **CPU cooler clearance:** up to 165mm
- **GPU length:** up to 380mm
- **Radiator support:** 360mm front, 240mm top
- **Drive bays:** 3× 3.5" + 1× 2.5"
- **Front I/O:** 2× USB 3.0, 1× USB-C, HD Audio
- **Included fans:** 3× 120mm ARGB fans
- **Stock OH:** 9
- **Key differentiator vs N7 Plus V2 (same price $139):** N4 Rev.1 has 380mm GPU clearance (vs 350mm on N7 Plus V2) — 30mm more for longer AIB cards. Also 3× 3.5" HDD bays (vs 2× on N7 and I3 Neo) — useful for storage-heavy builds. Same price at $139.
- **Summary at $139 Zalman tier:** Three models, same price: I3 Neo (ARGB fans + controller, 240mm radiator, no USB-C), N7 Plus V2 (USB-C, 360mm radiator, 350mm GPU), N4 Rev.1 (380mm GPU, 3× 3.5" HDD, USB-C, 360mm radiator). N4 Rev.1 is overall best-spec for GPU clearance and storage. N7 Plus V2 is best for AIO with standard GPU. I3 Neo is for entry ARGB showcase with no AIO.

## NZ Context Notes

- **Entry ATX ($89–$139):** Dominated by Zalman I3 Neo / N7 Plus V2 / N4 Rev.1 — all $139 with ARGB fans. I3 Neo is the volume seller. At $89, Segotep LUX 360 White is the cheapest ATX case with acrylic panel.
- **Mid-range ATX ($149–$229):** Jonsbo D41 range is the value leader ($119 mesh, $149 STD). Corsair 3000D Airflow RGB ($229) is for Corsair ecosystem buyers.
- **Premium ATX ($249–$579):** Lian Li Lancool 217 INF ($249) for quality-first builds. Jonsbo TK-4 ($329) for Sea View showcase. BO400 ($579) for aluminum-frame investment.
- **White cases:** Very limited — LUX 360 ($89 acrylic), Endura Pro+ ($115 EATX), VK03 Lite ($179), VK03 LCD ($219), Corsair 3000D ($229). All limited stock. White ATX at $139 doesn't exist in current range.
- **Hyperboloid glass is Jonsbo exclusive** — no other brand in this range offers curved/convex glass panels. TK-2 ($229) and TK-4 ($329) are the only options.
