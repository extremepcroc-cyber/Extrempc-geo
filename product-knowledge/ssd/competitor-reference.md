## Major NVMe SSD Brands (NZ Market Context)

### Tier 1 — Premium / Enthusiast
- **Samsung** — Gold standard. 990 PRO (Gen4) and 9100 PRO (Gen5) are top-tier consumer drives. Strong NZ presence and deepest single-SKU inventory.
- **WD Black SN850X** — Strong Gen4 alternative to Samsung 990 PRO (not currently in our active SKU list)
- **Crucial T700 / T705** — Gen5 alternative to Samsung 9100 PRO (Micron-made, strong reputation)
- **Kingston Fury Renegade / KC3000** — Premium Kingston tier (KC3000 is the established performer)

### Tier 2 — Mainstream
- **Predator (Acer) GM6 / GM7** — Strong value-premium with deep NZ inventory. GM7 is the higher-spec sibling.
- **HP FX900 Plus** — HP-branded mainstream with solid specs and reliability
- **Kingston NV3** — Value-tier mainstream (NV3 is the budget Kingston line, not the premium KC3000 family)
- **Team T-Force G50** — TeamGroup gaming-marketed value drive
- **Crucial P3 Plus / P5 Plus** — Crucial's value-tier (not in current stock)

### Tier 3 — Value / Budget
- **ADATA Legend 710** — Older Gen3 drive at budget pricing
- **OEM unbranded** — Bargain entry; useful for ultra-budget builds

## Specific NVMe SSD Comparisons (Current Stock)

### Samsung 990 PRO vs Predator GM7 (Gen4 premium tier)
- **990 PRO 2TB**: 7450/6900 MB/s, premium NAND, 5-yr warranty, gold standard reliability
- **GM7 2TB**: 7400/6500 MB/s, very close performance, lower NZD
- Both are excellent Gen4 picks. For brand-priority + maximum reliability, 990 PRO. For value-premium with slight cost savings, GM7.

### Samsung 990 PRO vs HP FX900 Plus (Gen4 premium tier)
- **990 PRO**: Samsung NAND, well-validated, premium-tier reputation
- **FX900 Plus**: HP-branded Tier 2, very close peak performance specs, slightly lower NZD
- Both are reliable Gen4 picks. Samsung has stronger overclocker/enthusiast community recognition.

### Kingston NV3 vs Team T-Force G50 vs ADATA Legend 710 (1TB value tier)
- **NV3 1TB Gen4**: 6000/4000 MB/s, mainstream value, broad NZ availability
- **G50 1TB Gen4**: 5000/4800 MB/s, gaming-marketed, similar tier to NV3
- **Legend 710 1TB Gen3**: ~2400/1800 MB/s, older Gen3 budget
- For Gen4 systems, NV3 or G50 (close call — pick by current NZD). For Gen3 systems or absolute budget, Legend 710.

### Samsung 9100 PRO vs Predator GM7 (Gen5 vs Gen4 flagship)
- **9100 PRO 2TB Gen5**: 14700/13400 MB/s — Gen5 flagship
- **GM7 2TB Gen4**: 7400/6500 MB/s — Gen4 flagship
- For Gen5 platforms (AM5 X670/X870, Intel Z790/Z890): 9100 PRO unlocks Gen5 bandwidth. For Gen4 platforms or where Gen5 isn't needed (typical gaming), GM7 saves meaningful NZD with near-zero gaming impact.

### HP FX900 Plus 4TB vs Predator GM7 4TB vs Samsung 990 PRO 4TB (4TB premium tier)
- All three are Gen4 4TB drives at premium tier
- 990 PRO is Samsung gold standard with longest reliability track record
- HP FX900 Plus has deepest 4TB inventory at ExtremePC (77 units)
- GM7 has limited 4TB stock (3 units)
- For brand priority, 990 PRO. For value premium with strong NZ supply, HP FX900 Plus.

## NZ Market Context

- NZ SSD prices have decreased significantly through 2024–2025 as NAND supply matured
- 2TB Gen4 NZD per GB is now competitive with 1TB (the sweet spot has shifted upward)
- Gen5 SSD pricing remains premium but is decreasing as platform adoption grows
- ExtremePC carries strong inventory across all tiers — particularly Samsung 990 PRO 2TB (139 units, deepest in NZ)
- For corporate / business builds, HP-branded drives satisfy procurement compliance requirements
- For value gaming builds, Predator GM6/GM7 family offers Tier 2 value-premium with strong NZ supply
