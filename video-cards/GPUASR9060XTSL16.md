## Comparison

- **vs ASRock RX 9060 XT 16GB Challenger OC (GPUASR9060XTCL16, value variant at lower NZD)** — Same chipset, same 16GB. Challenger has dual-fan, more compact (249mm vs 298mm), no RGB. Steel Legend has triple-fan, RGB, premium PCB components, reinforced frame. Choose Challenger for SFF + pure value; Steel Legend for thermals + aesthetic.

- **vs ASRock RX 9070 XT 16GB Challenger (GPUASR9070XTC16G, step up to higher tier)** — RX 9070 XT has 64 CUs (double), 256-bit bus, ~30–40% more performance. Worth the step up for 1440p ultra native gaming or 4K with FSR 4. RX 9060 XT is sufficient for 1080p ultra / 1440p with FSR.

- **vs NVIDIA RTX 5060 Ti 16GB (GPUMSI56TV2P, NVIDIA 16GB competitor)** — Same 16GB VRAM tier. AMD wins raster gaming ~5–10%; NVIDIA wins ray tracing and DLSS 4 Multi Frame Generation. For pure raster + VRAM, RX 9060 XT Steel Legend; for ray tracing + DLSS, RTX 5060 Ti.

- **vs Gigabyte RTX 5060 Ti WINDFORCE OC 8GB (GPUGIG5060TWFO8, NVIDIA 8GB at similar NZD)** — NVIDIA WINDFORCE has 8GB VRAM (half) but DLSS 4 + ray tracing advantages. For pure raster + VRAM headroom, RX 9060 XT 16GB wins decisively.

- **vs Colorful RTX 5060 Ti 8GB variants (multiple SKUs at lower NZD)** — Similar pattern — NVIDIA 8GB with ray tracing/DLSS focus vs AMD 16GB with raster/VRAM focus. Choose by primary workload.

## FAQ

- **Q: What does the "Steel Legend" branding actually mean?**
  A: Steel Legend is ASRock's premium AIB tier — sits above value-tier Challenger and below flagship-tier Taichi (not in current NZ stock). Adds reinforced metal frame, premium PCB components (2oz Copper, SP-Cap, Dr.MOS), triple-fan cooler, and Polychrome SYNC RGB. Engineering quality and aesthetic upgrades over the value tier.

- **Q: Will Polychrome SYNC work with my MSI / ASUS / Gigabyte motherboard?**
  A: Standalone Polychrome SYNC software controls the card RGB independently — works with any motherboard. For unified lighting sync with motherboard RGB, ASRock motherboard pairing is ideal. With other brands, you'll have two separate RGB control software (one for card, one for motherboard) but lighting still works.

- **Q: Triple-fan vs dual-fan — is the cooling improvement noticeable?**
  A: Yes — under sustained gaming load, expect 5–8°C lower GPU temps and measurably quieter fan operation. For Auckland summer ambient or builds with limited case airflow, the cooler upgrade is meaningful.

- **Q: What PSU do I need?**
  A: 550W 80+ Bronze minimum, 650W 80+ Gold recommended. Standard 8-pin connector — no 16-pin or ATX 3.0 PSU required.

- **Q: Will it fit a standard mid-tower case?**
  A: At 298mm length, fits all standard ATX cases (NZXT H7 Flow, Corsair 4000D, Lian Li Lancool 216). For SFF cases, verify max GPU length; the Challenger variant (249mm) is the SFF-friendly choice.

- **Q: 16GB VRAM — am I paying for VRAM I don't need at this tier?**
  A: Depends on use case. For 1080p ultra gaming, you won't see the benefit (8GB suffices). For 1440p ultra in modern AAA, the VRAM matters. For creative work (Stable Diffusion, video editing), 16GB is genuinely useful. The Steel Legend at this NZD positions the 16GB as both gaming future-proofing and creative work capability.

- **Q: Auckland summer heat — concerns?**
  A: Triple-fan cooler with air deflecting fin and reinforced metal frame handles 180W TDP in NZ ambient comfortably. Less likely to throttle than dual-fan alternatives.

- **Q: How does the LED switch work?**
  A: Hardware switch on the card lets you toggle ARGB RGB on/off without software. Useful if you want lighting off during productivity or video recording without disabling software RGB controls.

- **Q: How long is delivery to Wellington / South Island?**
  A: 1–3 business days via insured CourierPost from Auckland.
