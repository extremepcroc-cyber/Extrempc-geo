- **vs NVIDIA RTX 5060 Ti 8GB (multiple SKUs at lower NZD)** — RTX 5060 Ti has 8GB VRAM and DLSS 4 Multi Frame Generation. For pure raster gaming at 1080p, similar performance. For 1440p or VRAM-priority workloads, RX 9060 XT 16GB wins on VRAM. For ray tracing or DLSS-supported titles, RTX 5060 Ti.

- **vs NVIDIA RTX 5060 Ti 16GB (GPUMSI56TV2P, similar NZD tier)** — Direct competitor. Same 16GB VRAM. AMD wins raster ~5–10%; NVIDIA wins ray tracing and DLSS 4. For pure gaming with VRAM headroom, both are valid; pick by ecosystem preference and current price gap.

- **vs NVIDIA RTX 5060 (entry-tier NVIDIA)** — RTX 5060 is 8GB at lower NZD. RX 9060 XT is meaningfully faster with doubled VRAM. For 1080p ultra / 1440p builds, RX 9060 XT is the better choice; for pure budget builds with 1080p high, RTX 5060.

- **vs Previous-gen RX 7700 XT / 7800 XT (if still available)** — RX 9060 XT adds 3rd Gen RT (significantly improved ray tracing), 2nd Gen AI accelerators, FSR 4, and better efficiency. For raster gaming, close performance; for modern features, RX 9060 XT.

- **Q: AMD vs NVIDIA — which should I pick at this tier?**
  A: For pure raster gaming with 1080p ultra / 1440p target and existing PSU (no 16-pin), AMD RX 9060 XT 16GB. For heavy ray tracing (Cyberpunk path tracing) or CUDA-based AI/creative work (Stable Diffusion), NVIDIA RTX 5060 Ti. Both are excellent at this tier — pick by use case.

- **Q: What PSU do I need?**
  A: 550W 80+ Bronze minimum, 650W 80+ Gold recommended. Quality choices: Corsair RM650x, be quiet! Pure Power 12 M 650W, Seasonic Focus GX-650. Standard 8-pin connector — no 16-pin or ATX 3.0 required.

- **Q: How does FSR 4 compare to DLSS 4?**
  A: FSR 4 is AMD's ML-based upscaling launched 2025. Image quality is significantly improved over FSR 3 and approaches DLSS 4 Super Resolution quality. AMD doesn't yet have a direct equivalent to DLSS 4 Multi Frame Generation. For most titles, FSR 4 is competitive; for path-traced titles where Frame Generation matters most, NVIDIA still leads.

- **Q: Will it fit my SFF case?**
  A: At 249mm length and only 645 g, fits all popular SFF cases (FormD T1, NR200, A4-H2O, NCASE M2). Light weight minimizes sag.

- **Q: Is the AMD Linux experience really better than NVIDIA?**
  A: For Linux gaming via Steam Proton, AMD's AMDGPU + Mesa drivers are well-supported out of the box on Ubuntu, Pop!_OS, Bazzite, SteamOS. NVIDIA requires proprietary drivers with kernel module compatibility friction. For desktop Linux + Steam Proton gaming, AMD is the smoother experience.

- **Q: Does it work with FreeSync monitors?**
  A: Yes — FreeSync is AMD's adaptive sync standard and works perfectly with this card. FreeSync monitors are typically more affordable than G-Sync Compatible models, contributing to lower total build cost.

- **Q: Auckland summer heat — concerns with dual-fan cooler?**
  A: At 180W TDP, dual-fan is competent for sustained gaming with reasonable case airflow. For builds with constrained airflow or 28°C+ ambient, consider Steel Legend triple-fan variant.
