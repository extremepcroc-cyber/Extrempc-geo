## FAQ

- **Q: Do I really need 32GB VRAM?**
  A: For 4K maxed gaming alone: 24GB would have been enough; 32GB is headroom. For Stable Diffusion XL with massive batches / FLUX models / large local LLMs (70B+ params) / 8K video editing / Blender massive scenes: 32GB is genuinely useful and 24GB cards would force compromises.

- **Q: PSU requirements?**
  A: 1000W minimum (mandatory ATX 3.0 with native 16-pin), 1200W 80+ Platinum recommended for sustained max load and future upgrade headroom. Quality choices: Corsair AX1200i, Seasonic PRIME TX-1300, be quiet! Dark Power Pro 13 1300W. Skip 80+ Gold at this tier — Platinum saves Auckland summer heat dissipation.

- **Q: Will it fit my case?**
  A: WINDFORCE is a 3-fan, ~340mm long card. Requires full-tower or premium mid-tower (Lian Li O11 Dynamic XL, NZXT H9 Flow, Phanteks Eclipse G500A). Confirm case max GPU length spec.

- **Q: What CPU should I pair with this?**
  A: Don't pair with budget CPUs — wastes the GPU. Match with AMD Ryzen 9 9950X3D / 9950X (for productivity-heavy mixed workload), Intel Core Ultra 9 285K (for pure productivity), or AMD Threadripper PRO for HEDT workstations. Pure gaming-focused builds: Ryzen 7 7800X3D or 9850X3D as minimum.

- **Q: Auckland summer heat — sustained 575W?**
  A: Plan cooling proportional to the chip. Premium case airflow (4+ intake fans, 360mm radiator or equivalent), good cable management for unrestricted GPU intake. WINDFORCE handles sustained load well but ambient room cooling matters at this TDP.

- **Q: Will it work with my older PSU + 16-pin adapter?**
  A: Technically yes — Gigabyte may include a 4×8-pin adapter. But: a workstation-tier GPU on a non-ATX 3.0 PSU with adapter is unwise. The 16-pin (12V-2x6) under stress on older PSUs has been a known failure point. Upgrade to ATX 3.0 PSU with native 16-pin cable for reliability.

- **Q: How does RTX 5090 compare to AMD MI300X or similar AI accelerators?**
  A: AMD's data center AI cards (MI300X, MI300A) are not consumer products — they're for hyperscaler / data center deployment with different pricing, software ecosystem (ROCm vs CUDA), and form factors. For consumer / prosumer AI work, RTX 5090 is the answer.
