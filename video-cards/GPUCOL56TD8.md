## FAQ

- **Q: Is 200mm small enough for FormD T1 / NR200 / SFF cases?**
  A: Yes. FormD T1 supports up to 230mm GPU; NR200 supports up to 330mm; A4-H2O supports up to 322mm. Gaming DUO at 200mm fits all popular SFF cases comfortably.

- **Q: How does Colorful 3-year warranty work in NZ?**
  A: Warranty is handled through ExtremePC NZ domestically. RMA process is through ExtremePC — not Colorful directly. ExtremePC dispatches replacement / repair through NZ-domestic distribution.

- **Q: Will Auckland summer heat affect the compact 2-fan cooler?**
  A: At 180W TDP with the dual ring-shaped fans, the card runs warm but within thermal targets. For Auckland 28°C+ ambient days, ensure case airflow is reasonable (at least 2 intake / 2 exhaust fans). For builds with weak airflow, consider WINDFORCE 3-fan variant.

- **Q: What PSU do I need?**
  A: 600W minimum, 750W 80+ Gold recommended. Quality choices: Corsair RM750x, be quiet! Pure Power 12 M 750W, Seasonic Focus GX-750.

- **Q: Can I disable the auto-stop fan technology?**
  A: Yes via Colorful iGame Center software, but most users leave it enabled — fans off at desktop / idle saves wear and reduces noise.

- **Q: 8GB VRAM — is it enough for modern AAA?**
  A: At 1080p ultra, yes. At 1440p ultra, you'll hit the 8GB ceiling in modern titles (Hogwarts Legacy, Stalker 2, ray-traced Cyberpunk). For 1440p / future-proofing, consider 16GB variants.

- **Q: How does One-Key Overclock differ from this Gaming DUO?**
  A: Gaming DUO doesn't have the One-Key OC button (that's on the iGame Ultra and Vulcan series). Gaming DUO ships at factory boost (2572 MHz) — manual OC is possible via MSI Afterburner or similar tools.

- **Q: How long is delivery to Wellington / South Island?**
