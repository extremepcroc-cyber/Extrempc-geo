## FAQ

- **Q: How does Intel Arc B580 compare to NVIDIA / AMD at this tier — should I really consider Intel?**
  A: Yes — Battlemage is the first Intel Arc generation that's genuinely competitive at this tier. The 12GB VRAM is sweet-spot positioning, AV1 encoder is best-in-class, and drivers have matured through 2024–2025. For first-time NZ builders who don't have brand loyalty, B580 is a legitimate value pick. The caveats: heavy ray tracing favors NVIDIA; the AMD RX 9060 XT slightly outperforms in raw raster gaming.

- **Q: PCIe 4.0 x8 — will this affect my performance?**
  A: On modern PCIe 4.0+ motherboards (AM4 with X570/B550, AM5 with B650/X670/B850, LGA1700 with B660/B760/Z690/Z790, LGA1851 with B860/Z890): no real impact. On older PCIe 3.0 motherboards (B450 with old chipsets, LGA1200): expect ~5% performance loss. Pair with a PCIe 4.0+ motherboard for full bandwidth.

- **Q: Driver issues — is Intel Arc still rough?**
  A: The original Alchemist generation (A380/A750/A770) had a rough launch with DX9/DX11 game issues. Battlemage (B580) launched with mature drivers thanks to Intel's significant 2023–2024 driver work. Modern DX12/Vulkan titles run well. Some older DX9/DX11 games (pre-2015) may still have edge case issues — for an older-game-heavy library, NVIDIA/AMD have more mature support.

- **Q: AV1 encoder — why does this matter for streaming?**
  A: AV1 is a modern video codec that delivers ~30% better quality per bitrate than H.264. Twitch and YouTube increasingly support AV1 ingest. Intel's hardware AV1 encoder is widely regarded as the highest quality among consumer GPUs (better than NVIDIA NVENC AV1, AMD's AV1 encoder). For streamers chasing the cleanest possible stream, this is a meaningful advantage.

- **Q: Stable Diffusion / AI workloads on Intel Arc?**
  A: Possible via Intel OpenVINO + custom Stable Diffusion forks (SD.Next, etc.). More setup friction than NVIDIA CUDA's plug-and-play. If AI workloads are a serious priority, NVIDIA RTX 5060 Ti / 5070 are the smoother experience.

- **Q: What PSU do I need?**
  A: 650W 80+ Bronze minimum, 700W 80+ Gold recommended. Quality choices: Corsair RM650x, be quiet! Pure Power 12 M 650W, Seasonic Focus GX-650. Standard 8-pin connector.

- **Q: Will it fit my SFF case?**
  A: At 249mm length and 720g, fits all popular SFF cases (FormD T1, NR200, A4-H2O, NCASE M2). Light weight minimizes sag.

- **Q: XeSS 2 — does it work in my games?**
  A: XeSS is supported in a growing list of AAA titles (similar to FSR but smaller than DLSS). Cyberpunk 2077, Hogwarts Legacy, Marvel's Spider-Man, Death Stranding, and dozens of others support XeSS. For 1080p ultra → 1440p with XeSS, the upscaling is excellent.

- **Q: Auckland summer heat with the dual-fan cooler?**
  A: At ~190W TDP, dual-fan handles sustained gaming in NZ ambient with reasonable case airflow. Light at 720g, no sag concerns.
