## FAQ

- **Q: Will RTX 5060 8GB be enough VRAM for modern games?**
  A: At 1080p high settings, yes — current and near-future AAA titles fit comfortably in 8GB at this resolution. At 1440p ultra, you'll bump against the 8GB limit in titles like Hogwarts Legacy, Stalker 2, and ray-traced Cyberpunk. If 1440p is your target, consider RTX 5060 Ti 16GB instead.

- **Q: Does DLSS 4 work on all games?**
  A: DLSS 4 Multi Frame Generation requires game integration. Supported titles include Cyberpunk 2077, Alan Wake 2, Hogwarts Legacy, Star Wars Outlaws, Black Myth Wukong, and 100+ others. The list is growing monthly. DLSS Super Resolution (older DLSS feature) works in 700+ titles.

- **Q: What PSU do I need?**
  A: 550W 80+ Bronze minimum, 650W 80+ Gold recommended. Quality brands: Corsair RM650x, be quiet! Pure Power 12 M 650W, Seasonic Focus GX-650. For Auckland summer ambient temps, Gold-rated PSUs run cooler with longer fan life.

- **Q: Will it fit in my SFF case (NR200 / A4-H2O / FormD T1)?**
  A: Yes. The 2.5-slot, 227mm design is one of the smallest RTX 5060 cards available. All popular SFF cases listed accommodate it comfortably.

- **Q: Can I use my existing PCIe 4.0 motherboard?**
  A: Yes. RTX 5060 is PCIe 5.0 native but fully backwards compatible with PCIe 4.0 and even PCIe 3.0. Real-world performance loss in PCIe 4.0 mode is under 1%.

- **Q: How loud is it during gaming?**
  A: 0dB technology means fans are off at idle / desktop / video playback. Under gaming load, the dual Axial-tech fans are quiet — measurably quieter than reference design. Most users find it inaudible over case fans or headphones.

- **Q: Is the OC ("overclocked") factory tuning meaningful?**
  A: Yes — ASUS factory OC gives ~2–3% higher boost behavior vs reference RTX 5060. Stable, no warranty implications, no manual tuning required.
