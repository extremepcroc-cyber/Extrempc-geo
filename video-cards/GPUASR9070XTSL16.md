- **Q: Will Polychrome SYNC work with my non-ASRock motherboard?**
  A: Polychrome SYNC software controls the card RGB independently with any motherboard. For unified lighting sync with motherboard RGB, ASRock pairing is ideal. With other brands, two separate RGB control software (one for card, one for motherboard) — lighting works but software is fragmented.

- **Q: How much better is the Steel Legend cooler than the Challenger?**
  A: Under sustained gaming load: 3–5°C lower GPU temps and measurably quieter. The Steel Legend also has premium PCB components affecting power delivery quality, which can affect OC headroom and long-term reliability. For thermal-priority builds or builders planning to overclock, Steel Legend is the right choice.

- **Q: What PSU do I need?**
  A: 800W minimum (quality), 850W 80+ Gold recommended for sustained max load. Standard 2× 8-pin connectors. Corsair RM850x, be quiet! Pure Power 12 M 850W. No 16-pin or ATX 3.0 PSU required.

- **Q: Will it fit my case?**
  A: Steel Legend with triple-fan cooler typically 320mm+. Verify case max GPU length spec. Most mid-tower (NZXT H7 Flow, Corsair 4000D, Lian Li Lancool 216) and all full-tower cases accommodate it.

- **Q: 4K gaming with this card?**
  A: 4K ultra native: 60–80 fps in modern AAA. 4K maxed + ray tracing native: 30–45 fps. With FSR 4 upscaling: 4K + ray tracing reaches 80–100 fps. For 4K maxed native without upscaling, step up to RTX 5080.

- **Q: Stable Diffusion / AI workloads — is AMD viable at this tier?**
  A: AMD's ROCm / HIP for AI is improving but trails CUDA. For Stable Diffusion, AMD requires DirectML / ZLUDA / ROCm setup with more friction than NVIDIA's plug-and-play CUDA. If AI workloads are a serious priority, NVIDIA RTX 5070 Ti is the smoother experience.
