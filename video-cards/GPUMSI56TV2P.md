## FAQ

- **Q: Is 16GB really necessary for gaming today?**
  A: Depends on resolution and titles. At 1080p, 8GB handles modern AAA. At 1440p ultra, modern titles regularly exceed 8GB (Hogwarts Legacy ~10GB, Stalker 2 ~10–12GB, ray-traced Cyberpunk ~12GB). If you're 1080p-focused and not planning 1440p upgrade, 8GB is fine. If 1440p is current or planned, 16GB matters.

- **Q: Does the doubled VRAM affect performance or just memory capacity?**
  A: Same chip = same performance ceiling. VRAM is capacity, not speed. The benefit is removing memory bottleneck in VRAM-heavy scenarios — frame times stay smooth where 8GB cards stutter on texture loads.

- **Q: Can I run Stable Diffusion on this card?**
  A: Yes — SDXL and FLUX models run comfortably with 16GB. You can generate 1024×1024 images, do upscaling, and run ControlNets without VRAM compromise. 8GB cards force smaller resolutions or model splitting.

- **Q: How does this compare to RTX 4060 Ti 16GB (previous gen)?**
  A: RTX 5060 Ti 16GB has DLSS 4 Multi Frame Generation, GDDR7 (vs GDDR6), 5th-gen Tensor Cores, NVIDIA Reflex 2. Raw raster is ~15% higher. DLSS 4 is the meaningful gain in modern path-traced titles.

- **Q: Will it fit my SFF case?**
  A: Same 2-slot form factor as the 8GB VENTUS 2X. Fits FormD T1, NR200, A4-H2O comfortably.

- **Q: What PSU do I need?**
  A: 650W minimum, 750W 80+ Gold recommended. Corsair RM750x, be quiet! Pure Power 12 M 750W. Slightly higher PSU recommendation than 8GB variants due to memory power draw.

- **Q: 16GB at this tier — is this overkill for typical gaming?**
  A: For 1080p gaming, yes — you won't see the benefit. For 1440p ultra or any creative work, no — it's the sweet spot. Match VRAM to your actual use case.
