## FAQ

- **Q: Will RTX 5070 Ti handle 4K maxed without DLSS?**
  A: In current AAA titles at 4K ultra (no DLSS, no ray tracing): 60–80 fps achievable. At 4K + ray tracing native: 30–50 fps. With DLSS 4 Multi Frame Generation: 100+ fps in supported titles. For 4K maxed-out without DLSS, step up to RTX 5080.

- **Q: What CPU should I pair with this?**
  A: AMD Ryzen 7 7800X3D for pure gaming (best 1% lows), Ryzen 9 9700X / 9900X for mixed productivity, Intel Core i7-14700F or Core Ultra 7 265K for content creation. Avoid pairing with budget CPUs (5600X / 7500F class) — CPU bottleneck wastes the GPU.

- **Q: Will it fit my case?**
  A: VENTUS 3X is a 3-fan card — check case GPU length spec (typically 300mm+ needed). Most mid-tower and full-tower cases accommodate it. Compact SFF cases will not.

- **Q: PSU requirements?**
  A: 750W minimum (quality), 850W 80+ Gold recommended. ATX 3.0 PSU with native 16-pin (12V-2x6) preferred. Quality choices: Corsair RM850x, be quiet! Pure Power 12 M 850W, Seasonic Focus GX-850.

- **Q: My PSU has 8-pin only — can I use the adapter?**
  A: Yes — MSI includes a 16-pin adapter (typically 3×8-pin). Use a quality PSU with 3 free PCIe 8-pin cables. For cleaner builds, upgrading to ATX 3.0 PSU is recommended.

- **Q: How does 5070 Ti compare to AMD RX 9070 XT?**
  A: Close raster performance. NVIDIA wins ray tracing and DLSS 4 vs FSR 4. For mixed gaming + creative or modern path-traced AAA, NVIDIA. For pure raster gaming on a budget, AMD.

- **Q: Stable Diffusion / AI workloads — is 16GB enough?**
  A: For SDXL, FLUX, and most current AI image gen models: yes, 16GB is comfortable. For local LLM inference of 13B+ parameter models, you may want 24GB+ (RTX 5090).

- **Q: How long is delivery to Wellington / South Island?**
