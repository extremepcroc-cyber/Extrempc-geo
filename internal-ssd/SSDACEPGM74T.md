## FAQ

- **Q: 4TB — is this overkill for gaming?**
  A: For single-game-at-a-time players, yes. For users keeping 20–40 active games simultaneously, 4TB eliminates the "delete to install" cycle. Modern AAA games at 100–150GB each fill 2TB in 15 games; 4TB comfortably holds 30–40 active installations.

- **Q: GM7 4TB vs GM7 2TB — same speed, different capacity?**
  A: Yes — identical 7400/6500 MB/s, DRAM cache, and 5-year warranty. The 4TB is the capacity step-up within the same GM7 family. Pick by storage needs, not performance.

- **Q: Will a single 4TB NVMe SSD overheat in extended workloads?**
  A: Larger NAND capacity spreads writes across more cells, which can run cooler per cell under sustained load. Motherboard M.2 heatsink still recommended at 7400 MB/s sustained. For Auckland summer ambient (25°C+), heatsink prevents throttling.

- **Q: 5-year warranty on 4TB — what's the TBW endurance?**
  A: Predator does not always publish TBW for every capacity; 4TB drives typically have higher TBW than 2TB of the same series due to larger NAND. Check Predator product page for confirmed TBW spec.
