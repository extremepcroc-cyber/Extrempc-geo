## FAQ

- **Q: G50 vs NV3 — which should I pick?**
  A: For read-dominant workloads (gaming, OS boot), Kingston NV3 wins on 6000 MB/s read. For write-dominant workloads (content creation, file batching), Team G50 wins on 4800 MB/s write. For warranty length priority, G50's 5-year beats NV3's 3-year. For most users, very close call — pick by current NZD or warranty preference.

- **Q: T-Force gaming branding — does it add value?**
  A: Aesthetic only. T-Force is TeamGroup's gaming-marketed line; technical specs differ slightly from TeamGroup's general lines but the differentiation is positioning. For users not concerned with brand aesthetic, the underlying TeamGroup quality is the same across lines.

- **Q: DRAM-less vs DRAM?**
  A: For typical gaming and OS use, DRAM-less with HMB delivers ~95% of DRAM-cache performance. For heavy sustained writes, content creation, or workstation primary drives, DRAM is meaningfully better. G50 is DRAM-less — fine for mixed gaming + light content creation.

- **Q: Will it work in my motherboard?**
  A: Most motherboards from Intel 12th Gen+ or AMD Ryzen 5000+ have Gen4 M.2 slots. G50 boots in Gen3 slots at Gen3 speeds (no benefit on older platforms).

- **Q: 5-year warranty — is it lifetime?**
  A: 5 years is the typical premium NVMe warranty length (Samsung 990 PRO is 5 years). Some Tier 1 drives offer endurance-based warranty (whichever comes first). For most consumer use, 5 years is excellent coverage.

- **Q: Heatsink needed?**
  A: Motherboard built-in M.2 heatsink recommended for Gen4 drives. For Auckland summer ambient, the heatsink prevents thermal throttling.

- **Q: How long is delivery to Wellington / South Island?**
  A: 1–3 business days via insured CourierPost from Auckland.
