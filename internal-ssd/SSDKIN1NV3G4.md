## FAQ

- **Q: NV3 vs Kingston KC3000 — what's the difference?**
  A: NV3 is Kingston's value-tier mainstream line (DRAM-less, lower NZD). KC3000 is Kingston's premium line (DRAM, faster speeds, 5-year warranty, higher NZD). For mainstream gaming, NV3 is sufficient and saves meaningful NZD vs KC3000.

- **Q: 6000/4000 MB/s — is this fast enough for gaming?**
  A: Yes — at this speed tier, game load times are typically bottlenecked by CPU/RAM, not SSD. Difference between 6000 MB/s NV3 and 7000+ MB/s premium drives in actual gaming is typically <1 second per load. For gaming, NV3 is sufficient.

- **Q: DRAM-less — does it really not matter?**
  A: For typical gaming and OS use, real-world impact is minimal — HMB (Host Memory Buffer) gives ~95% of DRAM-cache performance. For heavy sustained writes, video editing scratch disk, or workstation primary drives, DRAM cache (HP FX900 Plus, Samsung 990 PRO) is meaningfully better.

- **Q: Will it work in my motherboard?**
  A: Most motherboards from Intel 12th Gen+ or AMD Ryzen 5000+ have Gen4 M.2 slots. NV3 boots in Gen3 slots too (at Gen3 speeds — no benefit on older platforms; Legend 710 is the better Gen3 match).

- **Q: 3-year Kingston warranty — what does it cover?**
  A: Manufacturing defects and premature failures. Kingston's warranty reputation is industry-leading; RMA process is straightforward through ExtremePC NZ.

- **Q: Heatsink needed?**
  A: Motherboard built-in M.2 heatsink recommended for Gen4 drives under sustained load. For Auckland summer (25°C+), the heatsink prevents thermal throttling.
