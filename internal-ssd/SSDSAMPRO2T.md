## FAQ

- **Q: Will Gen5 actually benefit my gaming?**
  A: For pure gaming today, marginal benefit. DirectStorage adoption is growing but most current games don't fully utilize Gen5. The bandwidth advantage shows in: large model loading (AI), video file transfers, dataset processing, future DirectStorage AAA titles. For pure gaming, Gen4 is sufficient.

- **Q: What motherboards have Gen5 M.2 slots?**
  A: Intel Z890 (Core Ultra 200 / Arrow Lake), some Z790 (Intel 13/14th Gen with Gen5 M.2 slot), AMD X870 (Ryzen 9000), some X670E (Ryzen 7000). Always confirm specific motherboard model has Gen5 M.2 slot (not all do).

- **Q: Will it work on Gen4 motherboards?**
  A: Yes — backward compatible, runs at Gen4 speeds (7000+ MB/s). But you're paying Gen5 premium for Gen4 performance. For Gen4 platforms, Samsung 990 PRO is the cost-effective choice.

- **Q: Heat — is Gen5 cooling a problem?**
  A: Yes. Gen5 SSDs generate substantial heat. Active cooling (motherboard heatsink + sometimes integrated fan on the M.2 slot, or 9100 PRO's own heatsink variant) is recommended. Bare drive in poor airflow can thermal throttle quickly under sustained load.

- **Q: 9100 PRO with vs without heatsink?**
  A: Samsung sells both. Without heatsink (this SKU) at lower NZD; relies on motherboard heatsink. With heatsink at higher NZD. For Z890/X870 motherboards with substantial M.2 heatsinks, bare drive is fine. For others, with-heatsink variant or aftermarket heatsink recommended.

- **Q: How does it compare to Samsung 9100 PRO 1TB / 4TB?**
  A: 2TB is the sweet spot capacity for Gen5 flagship. 1TB available at lower NZD; 4TB available at higher NZD. The 2TB sits at the value/capacity sweet spot for Gen5.
